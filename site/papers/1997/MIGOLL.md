XAVIER O. MONASTERIO

MY GOALS FOR THE NEXT TWO YEARS

My first and most important goal is to go on teaching to the best of my ability, particularly beginners and students who do not intend to pursue a professional career in philosophy. I think it especially important to offer those students a good opportunity to begin leading an "examined life" rather than going on suffering unreflectively and passively the influence of whatever trends happen to be popular. This is why, for the last several years, I have elected not to teach 400-level courses, which are professionally more attractive and less arduous to teach, and to teach often not one but two courses of Introduction to Philosophy per term.

My second goal is to continue my reflection on the way philosophy of religion is commonly taught, in the hope of finishing before my death the book on which I have been working for several years. Typically, to judge on the basis of numerous books of introduction to philosophy, the issues concerning God and religion are discussed and presented to students as matters of purely intellectual assent or dissent. That amounts, in my view, to mistaking the God talked about in the language of statements made by objective, disinterested spectators--which is the traditional language of philosophers--for the God in whom believers actually believe and to whom they pray. A very different approach seems to me necessary to do justice to religious issues in philosophy, if we are to avoid the risk of unfairly misleading the students into religious skepticism under the pretext of rationality.

The book I have undertaken to write has proven to be much more difficult than I originally imagined. I can honestly say that my exploration has resulted in hundreds of pages, and that I am still dissatisfied with the result. My past experience prevents me from promising satisfactory completion of this work within a given period of time. All I can promise is that I will continue working hard on this subject which I personally deem important and quite germane to the goals of a Catholic univesity.

   

January 1997
