      This very week a Marianist congratulated me on this  award  and  asked
me whether I had prepared my speech for this occasion.
I answered honestly that  I  had  not  given  it  a  thought.  Whereupon  he
proceeded to beg me not to prepare anything, because prepared  speeches  are
long and solemn and boring. I decided to follow  his  advice  and  was  very
comfortable with the idea until it dawned on me a few  minutes  ago  that  I
had to say something and I had no idea what to say, which of  course  ruined
my dinner in spite of the generous amount of scotch I had on my stomach  and
on my head.
In desperation, I grabbed the only idea that came  to  my  mind,  which  was
that I should be very careful in my speech not to  outsmart  myself  because
it is very dangerous. Since for the time being I  have  no  other  idea,  at
least I can explain how I got this salutary fear of outsmarting  myself.  It
came from a story of real life told to me by a priest...

      So, since I am not going to outsmart myself, what I will do
is to follow the venerable tradition established by some of my  predecessors
and simply tell some of my experiences at UD.

      First of all, why did I come to this  Catholic  Marianist  University?
Why did I choose it among so many others? The  answer  is  that  I  did  not
choose it and that I would have gone to any  university  foolish  enough  to
take me, but this was the only one.
Why foolish? Because I had never spoken English  before  coming  to  Dayton.
Not for nothing Dr. Baker used to introduce me as  "his  favorite  wetback,"
to Martha Baker's consternation.

      As for my relation with the Marianists, I have to say that it  started
on the wrong foot. Fr. Dombro. But years later, just before  his  death,  he
kneeled before me and begged my pardon. I must say that that,  for  me,  was
the beginning of my  appreciation  for  the  Marianists  and  the  Marianist
spirit.
