April 9, 1992

Philosophy Editor

Eerdmans, William B., Publishing Co.

255 Jefferson Ave.

Grand Rapids, MI 49503

Dear Philosophy Editor:

In 1985 Paulist Press published my book of Introduction to Philosophy, entitled TO BE HUMAN. Paulist Press has now notified me that their edition of my book is exhausted and that they will not reprint it. They are doing away with their very small division of philosophy to concentrate exclusively on their area of specialization.

The original edition of my book appeared in 1985 (5,000 copies, soft cover, 241 pages). Considering that Paulist Press has no direct connection with Departments of Philosophy and no specialized advertising capable of reaching that sector, the fact that the first edition is exhausted seems to me to be a reasonable success. I believe that things could work even better with a specialized publishing company like yours, particularly considering that my book already has some adepts in the college world.

I am enclosing a copy of the table of contents, of the preface, and of one of the chapters of TO BE HUMAN, as well as my assessment of the competition. Actually, since the approach I follow is significantly different from the usual ones, I do not think that my book would compete directly with other introductory textbooks you may have in the market, but would rather extend your market.

I f after reviewing the enclosed material you are interested in examining the entire book, I would be happy to send you a copy.

I look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,

Xavier O. Monasterio, Ph. D.
