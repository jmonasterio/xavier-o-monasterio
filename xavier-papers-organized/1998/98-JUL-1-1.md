DECISION AS PROSPECTIVE 'KNOWLEDGE'

In situation of encounter rather than of contemplation, where one is submitted to spatio-temporal conditions and one has to respond and act, where one has to "face the future by way of action" as Kierkegaard says, truth as correspondence or adequation or intentional identity doesn't apply, for there is no co-presence of mind to object, since the desired or aimed-at is future.

As in the case of the British captain.

There is a passivity. The fire is there to be responded to, for the actual exposure has already obtained. And there are regularities that create regular expectations as to what is impending and as to how to respond. And there are regular signs as to what is impending: objects that look similar sign similar effects. And past experience accumulates: what was originally a difficult task (as in driving a car) becomes a skill, a habit.

All this seems to belong in the area of knowledge. Yes, but of practical knowledge, where desire as a compnent ofction is of the essence, where the rules are only rules of thumb, where there is no rule to apply the rules, and where the exposed subject pays for his or her mistakes.
