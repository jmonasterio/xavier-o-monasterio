From: Xavier O. Monasterio
Re: CIT Seminar


Concerning purposes of the seminar

- Apart from such general purposes as enhancing  our  familiarity  with  the
Catholic intellectual tradition and having a number  of  people  participate
in dialogue around that  theme,  is  there  any  specific  purpose  to  this
seminar? For instance, are  we  supposed--as  a  group--to  put  together  a
concrete proposal for a cluster? If so, are we  to  make  of  this  specific
purpose the core around which readings and meetings are to be organized?
      Another  possible  specific  purpose:  examining  the  notion  of  CIT
implicit in the rationale of the pertinent  UD  documents,  criticizing  it,
clarifying it, proposing a better alternative.

Elements to consider and coordinate

- Readings
- Meetings                        COORDINATED PURPOSE OF EACH
- Cluster of courses
- Invited guests
- Papers by members
- Reason and faith as an issue?

Possibilities for meetings

-  Reflection  on  and  discussion  of  selected   readings   (preceded   by
instruction as to how to read or questions to  be  asked).  The  person  who
proposes a particular reading is responsible for explaining the  reason  for
that  particular  choice,  furnishing   questions   or   instructions,   and
facilitating the discussion (perhaps preceded by  a  short  presentation  by
the moderator).

- Or, apart from the list of original  sources  for  purposes  of  enhancing
one's Catholic culture, an additional list of short readings  (articles,  or
specific section of a long reading). Same procedure as above.

- Or instead of papers at the end of the seminar  (or  of  short  readings),
short communications by those who have something  to  say  for  purposes  of
reflection   and   discussion.   Twenty    minutes    (ideally    prewritten
communications)  on  particularly  important  aspects  of  CIT,  of   issues
involved, of elements of solution.

Concerning readings

- Whatever the readings chosen, the main reason why each of the readings  is
chosen  should  be  given.  Why  original  sources  rather  than   secondary
literature? The choice will depend on the fundamental purpose  of  assigning
readings: To enhance our Catholic culture? To be the basis for each  of  our
meetings?

      In any case, and depending on  the  purpose  for  which  readings  are
chosen and assigned, the order of the readings  (chronological?  thematic?),
the schedule for at least the first half of the seminar,  and  the  list  of
facilitators or communicators should be determined.
