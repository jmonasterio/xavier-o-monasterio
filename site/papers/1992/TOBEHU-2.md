March 10, 1992

Philosophy Editor

Paragon House

90 Fifth Avenue

New York, NY 10011

Dear Philosophy Editor:

I have been notified by Paulist Press that the first edition of my book of Introduction to Philosophy, entitled TO BE HUMAN, is exhausted, and that they will not reprint it. They are doing away with the very small division of philosophy they used to have in order to concentrate exclusively on their area of specialization.

The original edition of my book appeared in 1985 (5,000 copies, soft cover, 241 pages). Given that Paulist Press has no direct connection with Departments of Philosophy and no specialized advertising capable of reaching that sector, I consider the fact that the first edition is exhausted a reasonable success. It is my conviction that things could be much better with a specialized publishing house like yours, particularly considering that my book has already some adepts in the college world.

I am enclosing the table of contents, the preface, and one chapter of TO BE HUMAN. In addition, I am enclosing a comparison between my book and the only somewhat similar book I can think of in today's market. If on the basis of these items you think you might be interested in considering my book for publication, let me know, and I will send you a copy.  

As the approach I follow in my book is significantly different from the usual ones and tends to appeal to different people, I think you would not be competing against any other introductory textbooks you may already have in the market, but, rather, extending your market.

I look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,

Xavier O. Monasterio, Ph. D.
