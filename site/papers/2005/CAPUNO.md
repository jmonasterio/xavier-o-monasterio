\wp\chantill\capuno July 29, 1991

The God of Philosophers and the God of Faith

Many reflective people, at least since Pascal's time, have sensed a radical difference between the God of whom philosophers talk and the God in whom people--at least Christians--believe. I share their concern.

The issue is not merely academic.

But neither is it an easy one. Nothing short of an historical elucidation will do. And for that we have to go back in time to the moment when the separation between the God of faith and the God of philosophers took place and became instituionalized. That moment was the thirteenth century, when Christian thinkers encountered the bulk of Aristotle's works and started understanding their faith in Aristotelian categories, as we still do to this day.

The eclipse of biblical narrative

In his justly celebrated book The Eclipse of Biblical Narrative Hans Frei analyzed the loss of the understanding of the Bible as a narrative in the exegetical domain.
