Essay. 8                                Chantilly, May 31, 93
  Theology: From "Historia" to "Scientia"

      The possibility  and the advisability of transforming theology into a
discipline capable of figuring in the Aristotelian map of the "sciences"
was explicitly and hotly debated between approximately 1230 and 1265.
Strange as this may seem to us, theology was identified at the time with
"sacra pagina," that is, with the text of the Bible. Concretely, then, the
question that was actually debated under the label "an theologia sit
scientia" or its equivalent "an sacra pagina sit scientia," which recurred
in all the theological "Summae" between the above mentioned dates, was
whether the Bible could without loss be couched in scientific terms and be
judged by the same standards as a scientific work of the type, say, of
Aristotle's Metaphysics.

      The hypothesis under which I will proceed is that the shift that took
place in the thirteenth century represents an early episode of the
transformation analyzed by Frei in The Eclipse of Biblical Narrative, at
the time of the Enlightenment, with a comparable loss.
