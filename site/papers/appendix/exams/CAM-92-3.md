(1) Imagine a slave who revolts against his master and who, having become powerful, enslaves his former master. Would Camus consider the conduct of this slave as an instance of what he calls "rebellion"? Explain why, or why not.

(2) What is the central problem or question with which Camus deals in The Rebel , and what is his solution or answer to it in that book?

(3) Imagine that Camus were hiding a Jew in Nazi Germany. Given Camus' position concerning the justification of the means by the end, would he lie to the Nazis to save the life of a Jew? If not, how does Camus' position differ from Tarrou's? If yes, how does Camus position differ from Monasterio's?

4) Is Stepan's position concerning means and ends the same as

Camus' position in his first period? Explain why, or why not.
