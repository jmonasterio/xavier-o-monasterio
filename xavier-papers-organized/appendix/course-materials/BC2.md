                          The Contract

Contract:

 1.  Was the target behavior clearly specified?  Yes

      During our first meeting Steve and I agreed that he would complete five more problems in a twenty minute period to receive ten minutes of free time.  He agreed to completing the task with 80 % accuracy.

 2.  Did the contract provide for immediate reinforcement?  Yes

After completion of his assignment he was told to bring the assignment to me for grading.  After this he received his reinforcement.

 3.  Was the reinforcement frequent and in small amounts?  Yes

For the amount of effort and time Steve received frequent and small reinforcements.  I also provided social reinforcement during the task (i.e. you're doing great).  Generally despite his problems in math Steve finished his assignment sooner than the twenty minute time allotment (the time was renegotiated later).

 4.  Did the contract reward accomplishment rather than                obedience? Yes

      Steve was rewarded for his completion of progressively more problems.  At first I was more concerned with completion of more than his consistent 10 problems, and because of this Steve was rewarded for accurately completing at least 15 problems which he did with little difficulty.  He was rewarded for his attempts as well as his accuracy as seen in the criterion level of 80%.

 5.  Was the individual rewarded after the behavior occurred?       Yes

He received his reward after his completion of 15 problems.

 6.  Was the contract fair? Yes

It did not benefit me any more than it benefitted Steve.  Other children in the class work on a reward system so no apparent jealousy was seen form the other children.

 7.  Were the terms of the contract clear? Yes

Steve knew what he had to do to receive the reward.  He suggested the ratio and rewards in the contract, and participated in the activity (behavior) that would get him his reward.

 8.  Was the contract honest? Yes

We were both prepared to renegotiate if the terms of the contract were not being fulfilled or understood.

 9.  Was the contract positive? Yes

A positive behavior was gained from the contract and the terms were agreed upon were in no way negative.

10.  Was the contract as a method used systematically? Yes

It was used in his daily math assignments.  He was reinforced every time he succeeded in the agreed task.  Later this method could be applied to help in the completion of all of his assignments.

11.  Was the contract mutually negotiated? Yes

Steve helped figure out suggestions for tasks, reinforcers, and helped in the actual writing of the contract.

12.  Was there a bonus clause? Yes

I played his favorite board game with him and added extra minutes to his free time.    

13.  Was there a penalty clause?

     Too punitive?

Steve had to complete the math problems with a certain amount of accuracy.  When he did not accomplish this he had to re work a specified amount depending on how many he agreed to accomplish that week.  I don't believe either of us thought this punishment was too harsh.  In fact it was motivating for Steve.

The Client

 1.  Did the client understand the contract? Yes

He did his assignments as specified and did ask for renegotiation sessions which I understood to mean he understood this was a contract that could be negotiated.

 2.  Is the client getting the reinforcer from another source?

I am the one who graded his papers and gave him his reinforcer.  His parents did notice a change in that Steve was doing better in math and getting his assignments done.  They were pleased and praised him.

 3.  Should the reinforcers be reevaluated?

The reinforcers we chose worked and when I noticed a "boredom" or a stand still in his progress, they were varied.

 4.  Has a problem behavior developed that may prevent the       initial behavior from occurring?

Steve sometimes worked the problems too quickly just to get his reward, but realized that his speed only delayed the reward due to his incorrect answers.

The Measurement

 1.  Can the data be verified as being accurate? Yes

His papers were graded and his progress was graphed.

 2.  Is the data collection task too difficult or complex? No

Steve's progress was easy to graph and follow.

Problems:

What are some problems that you might anticipate and how might you resolve them?

I anticipated that Steve might get bored with his reinforcers so I made sure that we made a rather extensive list so that the reinforcers could be changed periodically.  I also anticipated that the task would become too simple so I made it clear to steve that renegotiations would be made as needed until Steve was completing the entire math assignment.

Did you discover some "short cuts"... during the contract?

I discovered that varying the reinforcers every two days or so made a difference in Steve's effort and completion of his assignment rather than waiting until the end of each week.

Did you need assistance?  A mediator?

I did not need assistance other than his teachers suggestions of what might be good reinforcers.
