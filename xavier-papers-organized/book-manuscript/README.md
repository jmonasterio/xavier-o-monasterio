# Book Manuscript - Untitled

This folder contains the **8 chapters** of a book manuscript Xavier was working on between 1991-2000, plus **3 posthumously reconstructed chapters** (see below).

The book focuses on **Aquinas, Aristotelian theology, and the nature of faith**.

## Chapters (Xavier's originals)

1. **Determinatio Distrahens** (1997) - Aquinas's submission of the Bible to Aristotelian science
2. **Aquinas's Synthesis: Half Aristotelian, Half Christian?** (1997) - The Aristotelian revolution in medieval theology
3. **From Christian Meditation to Summa Theologica** (1997) - Anselm's "credo ut intelligam" and the rise of the quaestio
4. **On the Scientific Character of Theology** (1997) - Whether theology is a science
5. **The Notion of Faith** (1997) - Faith in the Fathers vs. Aquinas's scientific undertaking
6. **The Object of Faith** (1997) - What is believed in the faith-relationship
7. **Revelation, Theology, and the Bible** (2000) - Bonaventure and Aquinas: two syntheses
8. **Biblical Categories and Aristotelian Categories** (1997) - Incompatibility between faith and the Aristotelian framework; ends in working notes

## Reconstructed Chapters (not by Xavier)

Xavier's manuscript breaks off in chapter 8, whose final pages are working notes rather than finished prose. Chapters 9-11 are a **posthumous reconstruction** (2026), drafted with AI assistance from Xavier's own outline in the chapter 8 notes and from his closely related essays (REVELAT, 1990; NARRATIV, 1990; FREI-APP, 1993). They attempt to complete the argument as he projected it, in his manner. **They should not be quoted or cited as his writing.** Each carries a curator's note and a .notes file documenting its sources.

9. **Revelation as Self-Revelation** (reconstruction) - Revelation as God's self-revealing interaction, not transmission of knowledge
10. **Faith as Consent** (reconstruction) - The primacy of consent over assent; the Fathers' credere in Deum recovered
11. **The First Eclipse** (reconstruction) - The 13th-century roots of Frei's "eclipse of biblical narrative"; what theology can legitimately be

## Main Argument

Xavier's book argues that:
- Aquinas attempted to fit Christian faith into an Aristotelian epistemological framework
- This resulted in a **denaturation of Christian faith** under pressure from Aristotelian categories
- There is a **radical incompatibility** between Biblical narrative and Aristotelian propositional logic
- The medieval synthesis, while brilliant, fundamentally distorted the nature of revelation

## Files

Each original chapter derives from Xavier's WordPerfect files (CHAPUNO through CHAPEIGH in the source archive):
- `chapter-N.md` - Markdown conversion (most accessible)
- `chapter-N.notes` - Provenance and conversion metadata

The markdown conversions of chapters 1-8 received a light editorial pass (2026): conversion artifacts, misspellings, doubled words, and citation typos were corrected; Xavier's style, wording, and argument were left untouched.

## Scholarly Significance

This unpublished manuscript represents Xavier's sustained engagement with:
- **Medieval philosophy** (Aquinas, Bonaventure, Anselm)
- **Philosophy of faith and revelation**
- **Aristotelian epistemology and its theological impact**
- **Biblical hermeneutics**

The work critiques the Thomistic synthesis from within the Catholic tradition, arguing for a return to pre-Aristotelian (patristic) understanding of faith.
