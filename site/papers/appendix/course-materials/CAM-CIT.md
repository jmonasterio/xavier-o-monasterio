II. EVALUATION ITEMS

A. Course Objectives and Rationale

  

  1. Student Outcomes

a. In systematic confrontation with the existentialist    positions concerning God's existence, the absurdity    of life, human freedom, and morality, students will    have the opportunity to reconsider or develop their               own positions. The Catholic perspective on the

       above matters will be specifically presented to the    students, as the philosophers themselves treated in    the course frequently define their stances by

       contrast to the Catholic or at least the Christian

   tradition.

b. Students will gain first-hand acquaintance with

   one important philosophical school and a few

   influential modern philosophers. .

c. Students will be able to identify the main      philosophical tenets characteristic of    

       Existentialism by contrast to other philosophical

   schools.

c. Students will gain some understanding of the  

   relationship between philosophy and literature.
