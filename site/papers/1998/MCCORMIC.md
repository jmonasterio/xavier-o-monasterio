\wp\98\McCormick

- p. 15: The issues around which dissent (a dissent that erodes faith) occurs: Celibacy of the clergy, ordination of women, divorce, and contraception.

In a certain way, it does erode the faith of people in what was conceived as "the teaching Church." Ad hoc 'casuistry' about infallibility.

But shouldn't it erode...? Not anymore, because things have changed and the Church has backtracked on many things? Highly confusing and disconcerting, which tends to erode the faith of people.

Possible important Distinctions: - Matters of faith - Matters of mores - Matters of discipline - Matters of prudence, which have to do with general and with individual circumstances     

 - Teaching with pastoral authority - Teaching with the authority of a recognized expert on a subject (of faith, of mores, of discipline, of prudence (Christian prudence)?

- Dissenting (disagreeing, arguing against, propagandizing against, disobeying privately and in conscience, disobeying publically, inciting to rebellion [De Lubac, Congar].

- Precisely because most matters are 'disputable' in the sense of not infallibly defined or belonging to the articles of faith (quod semper, quod ubique, quod ab omnibus), what right has one to demand that one's preference be chosen?

- The pastoral of confession and communion in matters of conscience and in internal forum.

- The pastoral of confession and communion that excludes "sinners" from communion,d from full participation in the community's life of faith, and that tends to ekrode their life of faith and prayer in the long run.
