\wp\monas\bible-6

P. 10: Realistic-exposed reading of the central narrative  portions  of  the
Bible, not as proposition-statements suceptible of truth and  falsity.  Thus
the  Bible  was  not  seen,  practically,  as  source  of  truth,  i.e.   of
propositional truth, although theoretically--when it was read as a book,  as
a document--it may have been interpreted as source of  propositional  truths
on the baksis of a picture-theory of truth.

Pp. 11-12: Totally new notions of reason and revelation when they were  seen
as  complementary  sources  of  propositional  truth  for   which   evidence
(sufficient reason) could be furnished. Before,  there  were  no  reflective
distances. Hence no question of subordination. The autonomy  of  reason  was
inconceivable. The question of autonomy coud not even be raised.

P. 13: The notion of two sources implies the distinction between  revelation
and reason: logical distance between biblical world and rational world. -  A
totally  different  universe  of   discourse   between   Aquinas   and   his
predecessors (See p. 18).

P. 19: Collision course: authority vs vision. Plus common ground  as  source
of possible conflict.

Pp. 30-31: Chez St Augustin,  already  practical  (though  not  theoretical)
detachment.   Theological   questions   independent,   not   mere   biblical
commentary.

P. 31:  With  two  sources,  reason  coud  not  anymore  be  simply  ancilla
theologiae. And the synthesis was condemned to fail in favor of reason,  for
goes with vision and faith with blind fideism or emotivism (see p. 32).

THE POINT IS TO KNOW WHO KNOWS AND  WHO  THINKS:  THE  CURIOUS  CONTEMPLATOR
THIRSTY FOR TRUTH, OR THE EXPOSED PARTICIPANT.
