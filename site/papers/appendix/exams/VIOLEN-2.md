                 Albert Camus on the Legitimacy of Violence

                                  Abstract

      To few, if any, issues did Camus devote more  attention  than  to  the
issue of the moral legitimacy of the use of violence.
This paper follows his evolution concerning that problem.

      Once Camus emerged from his period of absurdism in which  all  actions
are equivalent (1937-1940), he took three successive stands  concerning  the
use of violence: Violence is sometimes justified  (1941-1945),  violence  is
unjustifiable  (1946-1947),  violence  is  sometimes  necessary  but   never
justifiable.

      The last section of the paper is devoted to a  critical  appraisal  of
Camus' final stand.
