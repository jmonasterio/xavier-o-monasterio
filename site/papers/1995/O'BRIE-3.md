Session of CIT on "SHAPING THE FUTURE OF THE CIT AT U. D."

The first part of the session  of April 28, 1995 (from 1.00 to 4.00 P. M.) will be divided in three periods of approximately one hour each.

In the first period we will discuss chapters 3, 4, and 5 of O'Brien's From the Heart of the American Church: Catholic Higher Education and American Culture . These chapters are for the most part historical; they deal with American Catholic education before the Sixties and the changes that have taken place since.

In the second period we will discuss chapters 6 and 8 of O'Brien's book. These chapters reflect on the problems we face today in American Catholic Colleges and Universities and suggest alternative directions for the future.

In the third period we will concentrate on the CIT at U. D., particularly on the cluster on the Catholic intellectual tradition.

Assuming that not everyone might have the time to read  O'Brien's book in its entirety, we have prepared a relatively detailed summary of the chapters that will not be explicitly included in our discussion, so as to insure that everybody will be aware of the whole context and will take the time to read  thoroughly the five chapters that have been selected.

For each of the chapters selected for discussion we formulated a few questions that we believe will be helpful for our reflection on the future of Catholic Higher Education at U. D.
