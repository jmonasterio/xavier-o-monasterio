1) Does Camus, in his second period, still think that life is absurd? If so, what is the difference between his first and his second period? Use The Plague to illustrate your answer.

2) In the Introduction to The Rebel Camus says that "we are living in the era of premeditation and the perfect crime." Explain what he is talking about. Why "premeditation"? Why "perfect crime"?
