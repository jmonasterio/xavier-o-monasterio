Xavier O. Monasterio

SECTION A - 1

What I intended to accomplish this summer was stated as follows on pages 4-5 of the project I submitted with my application:

"If I succeed in establishing that the opposition's objections to the adoption of the Aristotelian scientific model can legitimately be couched in terms of speech acts and narrative theory, and n making persuasive their force when couched in this way, the limited project I hereby submit for funding will be successfully completed."

The first step to achieve this goal consisted in collecting the pertinent material from the original medieval sources. It was carried out at the library of the Cetre Culturel "Les Fontaines," at Chantilly, Frnce, and completed by June 10.

The second step consisted in the actual writing of a persuasive account of the validity of the objections of the medieval theologians who maintained that the demands of the Aristotelian conception of science could not apply to theology, in particular because of the literary character of the biblical

books. I have not completed this part of the project. However, I have already a substantive draft of two papers which I expect to complete in the course of this year and submit for presentation and criticism in specialized conferences. I thekrefore think that I am reasonably close to the schedule I had set for my work.
