COURSE PROPOSAL FOR HUMAN CONDITION CLUSTER

I.  INFORMATION ITEMS

A. Course Title and Number : PHL 360 Existentialism

B. Number of Credit Hours : 3

C. Catalog Description :

  

       Major themes in representatives of the existentialist     movement, such as human freedom, the absurdity of human     existence, the primacy of action and the roles of        speculation and the emotions.

D. Prerequisite : None.

E. Available Faculty and Faculty Qualifications for this

   Course :

  Dr. Joseph Kunkel.

  Has some expertise in existential psychology. Has taught   this course in the past.

  Dr. Kurt Mosser.

  Nietzsche figures in his area of competence. Has         published on Nietzsche.

  Dr. Xavier Monasterio.

  Has taught this course at least once a term for the

  last twenty-seven years. Has published a number of        articles on Jean-Paul Sartre and Albert Camus.

F. Identify those academic units that might avail themselves    of this course :

  Philosophy, Religious Studies, International Studies.

G. Date Course Approved by College or School :

  Already in catalog.

II. A. Course Objectives and Rationale :

1. Students Outcomes

  a. Students will contrast the traditional philosophical

approach and the existential approach to such problems as human freedom, moral obligations, the individual

and society, and the meaning of life, among others.

  b. Students will learn to appreciate the difference that approaching these problems through literary works

rather than just through philosophical essays makes.

  c. Students will develop the ability to see

 philosophical issues as practical rather than merely

 theoretical problems.

  

  d. Students will enhance their ability to take their own philosophical stands in a responsible, critical way.

2. How do these outcomes meet General Education policy       requirements?

  This course advances the following General Education

  Goals:

  a. To reflect about the human condition in dialogue

with others.

  b. To develop the ability to deal with ethical issues

in a constructive way.

  c. To integrate literature and philosophy.

3. Is the course part of a General Education Cluster?

  Yes, the Human Condition Cluster.

  

B. Supporting data

  1. Detailed Topical Outline

a. The meaning of life.

   Study and discussion of such works as Camus' The        Myth of Sisyphus and Caligula , Sartre's Nausea and    some sections of Being and Nothingness , Marcel's

    Being and Having ...

b. Moral obligations.

   Sartre's "Existentialism is a Humanism," Camus'

   "An absurd reasoning" and The Plague , Buber's I and     Thou , Nietzsche's selections...

c. Freedom.

   Some sections of Sartre's Being and Nothingness ,

   Dostoievsky's The Underground Man ...

d. Individual and society.

   Camus' The Stranger and The Just Assassins ,        Malraux's The Human Condition , Marcel Man against

    Mass Society ...

 2. Teaching Methodologies :

Lectures.

Class discussion.

Small-group discussion.

Student presentations.

Film presentation and discussion.

 3. Procedures for Evaluating Student Performance :

Student's participation, individual presentations.

Paper.

Quizzes and exams.

 4. Text(s) and Supplementary Materials :

For some examples, see above: II, B.

for a particular course may change from term to term

and from instructor to instructor.
