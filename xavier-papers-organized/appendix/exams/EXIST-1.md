TYPE OF COURSE: Combination of lectures and discussion.

EVALUATION: Based on seven tests. Four of those tests (on the literary readings) will be worth 10 points each. The other three tests (on philosophical material) will be worth 20 points each. Your final grade will be determined according to the number of points you accumulated through the term, following this scale:

100-93 = A

                              92-85 = B

84-70 = C

69-60 = D

59-0  = F

PARTICIPATION: Required.

In the case of a sizable group, the only effective way I find of keeping track of the students' participation is by their attendance. Any student with six absences or less will be deemed to have participated satisfactorily . Starting with the seventh absence you will be penalized with the subtraction of two points from your final total for each absence . Any student with less than three absences will get a bonus of two points added to his/her final total .

If you fail to attend on an exam day, the first time you will have the opportunity to make up for it during the next two school days, but you will automatically lose five points. The second time there will be no opportunity to make up for the exam missed.

It is your responsibility to insure that your record of attendance is kept correctly. Thus, if ever you arrive late to class, contact the teacher immediately after class concerning this matter.

DATE FOR THE FIRST EXAM: Friday, September 20. For that exam you are supposed to have read The Stranger thoroughly.
