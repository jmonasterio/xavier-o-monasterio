EXISTENTIALISM

BROAD SENSE :

"Existentialism" is the name for a particular philosophical approach characterized by the following features:

(1) The central focus of the Existentialists is the human subject as a free and responsible individual , by contrast to traditional philosophers who focus on being in general, one among which is the human being, and who therefore do not deal specifically with our individuality. ("Existentialism is a humanism," existentialists are accused of subjectivism--because they do not see the human being as object but as subject).

(2) By contrast to traditional philosophers who philosophize from the point of view of the detached observer (in imitation of the natural sciences), the Existentialists philosophize from the standpoint of the involved participant , which is out standpoint in life, the subject's standpoint. ("Is the branch going to break?").

(3) By contrast to traditional philosophers for whom philosophy is a purely theoretical discipline whose point is to know the truth, for the Existentialists philosophy is a practical discipline whose point is to act appropriately .
