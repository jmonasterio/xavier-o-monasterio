THE MEDIEVAL CONTROVERSY

The use of reason within faith had preexisted the second and third entries of Aris (cf. Corbin and Van Steenberghen). But it had been subordinated to faith and to a historical comprehension of faith. What changed radically was an autonomous understanding of reason, and an understanding of reason that rendered impossible the historical comprehension of faith . Furthermore, it made the opposition between reason and faith irreconcilable, at least on the part of reason, for Aristotelian reason is all-encompassing and autonomous.
