NAME__________________

1) Camus despises the society that kills Meursault, and admires Meursault who kills a man. Does Camus contradict himself? Explain why, or why not.

2) Would Camus say that a dog that eats, copulates, and sleeps, that is, a dog that leaves according to its preferences, is "authentic"? Explain why, or why not.

3) Why does Camus call religious faith "philosophical suicide"?

4) Camus praises Galileo for having said, when pressured by the Inquisition, that the sun turns around the earth, which was contrary to Galileo's convictions. Does Camus contradict himself, given that the demands that people be authentic?

5) In a way, Camus finds the discovery of life's absurdity exhilarating rather than crushing.

Is the above statement correct? If so, explain why Camus finds that discovery exhilarating. If not, give at least one reason to show that the statement is incorrect.
