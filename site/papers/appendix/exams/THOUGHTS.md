Selected "Thoughts"

The following selection of Pascal's Thoughts does not necessarily contain the most important ones. For that purpose, you are invited to browse on your own through the whole book and make your own selction. The purpose of this one is to help those of you who will not have the time or the patience to read the whole book. These are highly representative thoughts.

- 110 (p. 58): "Reason" and "Heart."

- 131 (pp. 62-66): The dual nature of man. (A typical example of Pascal's rhetoric).

- 149 (pp. 76-80): "I do not demand of you blind faith."

- 199 (pp. 88-95: We are something. We are not everything. That is what makes us incapable of certain knowledge and of absolute ignorance. (Another example of Pascal's rhetoric).

- 200 (p. 95): "Man is only a reed..., but he is a thinking reed."

- 243 (pp. 103-104): Typical of Pascal's view of other religions.

- 286-289 (p.120): Typical of Pascal view of Jews.

- 380-382 (pp. 138-139): On faith.
