NAME___________________

1) What does Sartre mean by forlornness? Explain whether or not this existential theme is present in Camus' first period.

2) Is Camus still an existentialist in his second period? If so, explain whether he is an existentialist in the strict sense or in the broad sense. If not, explain why he cannot be considered anymore as an existentialist.

3) Caligula thinks that truth and happiness are incompatible.

Does Camus, in his second period, agree with him? Use The Plague as the basis for your answer.

4) What is Camus' final judgment of Father Paneloux, as expressed by Rieux in The Plague ? Does this judgment imply that Christians do not care about what happens in this world, or that they commit philosophical suicide? If not, what are the reasons for this judgment? Explain.

5) Would Camus, in his second period, say that Meursault "won the match"? Explain why, or why not.
