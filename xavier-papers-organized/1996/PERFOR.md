XAVIER O. MONASTERIO PERFORMANCE REVIEW 1996

In the Fall Term I taught two courses of Introduction to Philosophy with a total of 73 students, and a course of Existentialism with 30 students. Total number of students: 103.

In the Spring Term, as usual, the quantified results of my students' evaluations were above the Department's average. I haven't yet received the results of the Fall Term.

SCHOLARSHIP

I have two articles already accepted for publication. One, entitled "Sartre and the Existential Approach," was selected for an anthology devoted to Sartre's existentialism edited by William McBride of Purdue University, to be published by Garland. I have been notified that it will appear this month of January. The other article, entitled "Santoni on Bad Faith and Sincerity: A Vindication of Sartre," is forthcoming in Sartre Studies International .

I submitted a paper entitled "Camus' Meditation on the Christian Faith" to the American Catholic Philosophical Quarterly .

It is presently being refereed.

I directed two sessions for the faculty who participate in the Cluster on Faith and Reason; they were devoted to the discussion of an extensive paper I wrote for that specific purpose. My colleagues' reactions were enthusiastic.
