# Book Manuscript - Untitled

This folder contains what appears to be **8 chapters** of a book manuscript Xavier was working on between 1997-2000.

The book appears to focus on **Aquinas, Aristotelian theology, and the nature of faith**.

## Chapters

1. **Determinatio Distrahens** (1997) - Aquinas's submission of the Bible to Aristotelian science
2. **Aquinas's synthesis: Half Aristotelian, Half Christian?** (1997) - The Aristotelian revolution in medieval theology
3. **Saint Anselm: Toward a new theological approach** (1997) - Anselm's "credo ut intelligam"
4. **On the Scientific character of theology** (1997) - Whether theology is a science
5. **The Notion of Faith** (1997) - Faith in the Fathers vs. Aquinas's scientific undertaking
6. **The object of Faith** (1997) - What is believed in the faith-relationship
7. **Bonaventure and Aquinas: Two syntheses** (2000) - Comparing two medieval approaches
8. **Biblical Categories and Aristotelian Categories** (1997) - Incompatibility between faith and Aristotelian framework

## Main Argument

Xavier's book appears to argue that:
- Aquinas attempted to fit Christian faith into an Aristotelian epistemological framework
- This resulted in a **denaturation of Christian faith** under pressure from Aristotelian categories
- There is a **radical incompatibility** between Biblical narrative and Aristotelian propositional logic
- The medieval synthesis, while brilliant, fundamentally distorted the nature of revelation

## Scholarly Significance

This unpublished manuscript represents Xavier's sustained engagement with:
- **Medieval philosophy** (Aquinas, Bonaventure, Anselm)
- **Philosophy of faith and revelation**
- **Aristotelian epistemology and its theological impact**
- **Biblical hermeneutics**

The work critiques the Thomistic synthesis from within the Catholic tradition, arguing for a return to pre-Aristotelian (patristic) understanding of faith.
