Directed readings

1998-1999: Spring Term

Instructor: Monasterio

Title of Course: Jean-Paul Sartre

Description: An introduction to Jean-Paul Sartre's existentialist philosophy. The course will focus on the most important chapters of Being and Nothingness , supplemented with the study of the two plays of Sartre's existentialist period-- The Flies , and No Exit . Sartre's long article "Materialism and Revolution" as well as chapter 1 of his Search for a Method will be used to explain Sartre's transition from Existentialism to Marxism. Some explanatory secondary literature will also be used to guide the student through

the intricacies of Being and Nothingness .

Method of Evaluation: The student's notes on the readings and our weekly discussions will determine half of the grade; the other half will be determined by the quality of three short papers.
