TEXTBOOKS REQUIRED; A Brave New World , by Aldous Huxley

The Trial and Death of Socrates , by Plato

TYPE OF COURSE; Combination of lectures and some discussion

EVALUATION; Based on five exams. Two of those exams will be on                the literary readings; the first will be worth 15                 points, and the second 10 points. The other three            exams will be on strictly philosophical material and              each of them will be worth 25 points. Your final            grade will depend on the number of points you have              accumulated through the term and will be determined               according to the following scale:

                         100-92=A

                          91-85=B

                          84-70=C

                          69-60=D

                          59-0 =F

PARTICIPATION: Required

In the case of a sizable group like this one, the only effective way I find of keeping track of the students' participation is by their attendance.

Any student with six absences or less will be deemed to have participated satisfactorily .         

Starting with the seventh absence you will be penalized with the subtraction of two points from your final total for each absence.

Any student with less than three absences will get a bonus of two points added to his final total.

It is your responsibility to insure that your record of attendance is kept correctly. Thus, if ever you arrive late to class, contact the teacher immediately after class concerning this matter.

If you fail to attend on an exam day, the first time you willl have the opportunity to make up for it during the next two school-days , but you will automatically lose 5 points. The second time there will be no opportunity to make up for the exam missed.

DATE FOR THE FIRST EXAM: Wednesday, September 18. For that exam you are supposed to have read A Brave New World thoroughly.
