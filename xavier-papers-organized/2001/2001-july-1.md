2001-July-1                                                   July 3, 2001

          THE FUNCTION OF DOCTRINE IN FAITH AND THEOLOGY

      The important points to be made are:
(a) That doctrine is neither true nor false, for it is not a representation
or a misrepresentation of a sacred reality.
(b) Hence, the idea of “one truth” needs modification.
(c) Christian, Catholic doctrine takes primarily the form, not of a series
of statements, but of a sacred, ongoing, dramatic history with distances of
exposure, within which everything is situated and by reference to which
everything takes its appropriate place-value, much as Frei characterizes
the situation before the eclipse of biblical narrative. Of course, whether
or not one takes one’s place within that history depends on whether or not
one has faith. And, to begin with, one either has or has no faith. The
faith paradigm cannot be demonstrated. What can be made persuasive is
that the prevailing science paradigm doesn’t make sense.
