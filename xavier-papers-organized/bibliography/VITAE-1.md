                             RESUME

Xavier O. Monasterio
Department of Philosophy
University of Dayton
Dayton, OH 45469-2260

Birth

      Mexico City, Mexico, on January 17, 1926.

Education

      1964: Ph. D. in Philosophy, University of Paris, France.

       1951:       M.  A.  in   Philosophy,   Ysleta   College,   El   Paso,
      Texas.

      1947: Diploma in Classics (Latin and Greek), Instituto
                 Libre de Estudios Superiores, Toluca, Mexico.

Doctoral Dissertation

"La conception de la liberté chez Rodrigo  de  Arriaga:  Un  précurseur  des
Existentialistes" (Chairman: Jean Wahl).

Record of Employment

1966-Present:    University   of   Dayton   (currently   holding   rank   of
Professor in the Department of Philosophy).

      1990: Visiting Professor, Doctoral Program in Theology,
                 International Mariological Research Institute,
                 University of Dayton, Summer Term.

     1977:                  Visiting Professor of Philosophy (Summer Term),
           School of  Library  and  Information  Science,    University  of
           Pittsburgh.

1954-1959:      Editor,     writer,     translator,     Editorial      Buena
Prensa, Mexico City, Mexico.

1951-1954:  Professor of Latin and Spanish Literature,
                 Instituto de Estudios Superiores, Mexico.

Honors and Awards

- Recipient of Summer Research and Travel Grant from NEH, 1993.    Spent  at
The Centre Culturel "Les Fontaines," Chantilly, France.

- Lackner Award,  University of Dayton, January 1993.

- Award for Outstanding Teaching, College of Arts and Sciences of
  the University of Dayton, 1986.

- Recipient of four Summer Research Grants, the latest in 1991
  (spent at the  Centre  Culturel  "Les  Fontaines,"  Chantilly,    France),
      awarded by the Research Council of the University of   Dayton.
