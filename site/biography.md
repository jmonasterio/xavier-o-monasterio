## Biographical Overview

Xavier O. Monasterio (also Xavier Ortiz Monasterio, Javier Ortiz Monasterio) was a philosopher and long-serving professor in the Department of Philosophy at the University of Dayton, Ohio, United States.

**Born:** 1926, Mexico
**Died:** January 4, 2011, Mexico City (cancer)
**Education:** Ph.D. in Philosophy, University of Paris
**Career:** 34 years teaching philosophy at the University of Dayton
**Spouse:** Genevieve Monasterio (art historian and conservation specialist)

## Academic Formation

Dr. Monasterio held a **Ph.D. in philosophy from the University of Paris**, confirming advanced graduate study in Europe and a strong French academic connection. His doctoral work in Paris shaped his later scholarship on existentialism and continental philosophy.

## Family and Personal Life

Contributor information in *Marian Library Studies* identifies his spouse, **Genevieve Monasterio**, an art historian and conservation specialist who held:
- Master's degree in archaeology and art history from the University of Paris
- Diplomas from the École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts (Paris)
- Diploma from the International Art Restoration Institute in Rome
- Professional experience as assistant curator at the Musée Carnavalet (Paris) before moving to Dayton

The Monasterios resided at **324 Lincoln Park Blvd., Dayton, Ohio 45429** during  much of his tenure at the university. Previously they also lived at the Georgetown apartments in Kettering.

## Academic Career

**University of Dayton, Philosophy Department** (34 years of service)
- Long-serving faculty member from the later 20th century into the early 21st century
- Retired after 34 years teaching philosophy at UD
- Published peer-reviewed scholarship through 1999
- Active in departmental colloquia and academic events from the 1970s onward
- Taught introductory philosophy courses with emphasis on existentialism and human nature

## Key Publications

### Books

**To Be Human: An Introductory Experiment in Philosophy** (1985)
- Publisher: Paulist Press
- ISBN: 0809127040, 9780809127047
- 241 pages
- An undergraduate-level philosophical text exploring human identity and philosophical inquiry
- Used theories of contemporary thinkers and Aldous Huxley's *Brave New World* as pedagogical tools
- Later reprinted by University Press of America in the 1990s

**Para ser humano: Introducción experimental a la filosofía** (1987)
- Spanish-language edition
- Publisher: Universidad Iberoamericana, México
- Catalogued under "Xavier Ortiz Monasterio"
- Subject areas: Existentialism and Psychology

### Journal Articles

**"Camus' Meditation on the Christian Faith"** (Fall 1999)
- Journal: *Horizons*, Volume 26, Issue 2
- Pages: 280–295
- Affiliation: University of Dayton
- A scholarly analysis of Albert Camus' engagement with Christian faith in *The Plague*

### Conference Papers and Proceedings

**"Ought Cats Be Tuesday? Reflections on the Neutrality of Philosophers"** (1974)
- Published in: *University of Dayton Review*, Vol. 11, No. 1
- Proceedings of the 4th Annual Philosophy Colloquium
- Addressed whether philosophers should maintain neutrality or embrace ethical commitment

## Scholarly Focus Areas

Based on published works, Monasterio's research and teaching centered on:
- Existentialism (Camus, contemporary existentialist thinkers)
- Philosophy of human nature and identity
- Ethics and moral philosophy
- Philosophy pedagogy and accessible introductory texts
- Engagement with literature as philosophical inquiry

## Name Variants and Identity

- English publications: **Xavier O. Monasterio**
- Spanish-language catalogues: **Xavier Ortiz Monasterio**
- Mexican records and obituaries: **Javier Ortiz Monasterio**

His full name in Spanish follows the Mexican naming convention (Ortiz Monasterio), while his professional publications in the United States used the simplified form "Xavier O. Monasterio."
