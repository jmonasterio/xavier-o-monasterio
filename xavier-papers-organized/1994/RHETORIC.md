ARIS' RHETORIC AS A WORKD OF PHILOSOPHY

Illocutionary acts must be propositional.

This is based on a view of language according to which the function of language is to state true propositions, and there are simple uses of language called "constatives," so that other things we do with words are deviation s from the basic use, namely, telling the truth.

Rhetoric is not an act of assertion (because of inducements. Assertions compel assent by bare reference to world).

There is room for directives (?) declaratives (?) because proof is superior to emotional appeals, and arguments are neither automatically successful nor beyond criticism.

Basic distinctions:

performatives - constatives

assertives (by description) - directives (by action)

word - world

Declaratives bring dispute to a close within an institutional setting.
