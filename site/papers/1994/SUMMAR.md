In Search for a Method Sartre interprets marxism as the philosophy for our times and sees the survival of existentialism as a provisional phenomenon due to the deviation of contemporary marxism from Marx's marxism. In a later text Sartre reversed completely his interpretation of marxism, of existentialism and, consequenlty, of his own relation to both. I claim that the latter interpretation is correct and that this affects the value and the place of Sartre's mature philosophy.
