2007-Feb-1-Matthew’s word, or God’s word?

Mattthew’s word, of God’s word?

My friend Joe said: “What Mattthew wrote is not God’s word, it is Matthew’s word about God.”

I disagree.

Mathew didn’t write assertions about God. He wrote a narrative in which Jesus is the protagonist or principal agent or inter-actor and the other characters are secondary. Some of the latter believed in Jesus –Matthew was one of these– others opposed Jesus. The two groups met Jesus, that is, interacted with him. But only one of the groups met Him as he wanted to be met.

Jesus didn’t speak to them in propositions about himself. He acted and interacted with people in the way narrated by Matthew. A narrative is not composed of assertions. Assertions demand assent or dissent. Not so a narrative, and still less, an interaction. A lived interaction, when written, becomes a narrative, not a collection of assertions about.

The events narrated may be historical or imaginary and may be narrated as

historical or as imaginary, that is, to be accepted as historical or as imaginary, as having taken place within our dramatic time-space, or not.. Maatthew’s narrative is a confession of his interaction with Jesus as he lived it..
