Essay. 2

Essay 2. From the XIth to the XII century

Status quaestionis

Before the XIth century the Bible was the text. No distinction between scientific and religious reading of it. And it was the canon for the truth of views and the propriety of actions. There was no other, much less other independent world-view. Philosophy was logic, dialectic (RASHDALL).

In the XIth century, incipient division between philosophy and theology with the problem of universals (Roscellini), between scientific, rational, independent discourse, and independent discourse (that is, relation to the text and goal pursued in the relationship). Logic gets applied to the Bible. But the biblical worldview remains supremely authoritative, canonical. More pronounced distinction between the schola artium and the schola theologiae. One can promote oneself from one to the other, as Abelardus did by shifting to the Bible and the theological quaestiones. Still the Bible was the text (with the Fathers' commentary and the decreta conciliorum).

Things change in the XIIth century with Lombard's Sententiae. Now, two texts in theology (DENIFFLE AND NON-DENIFFLE). Simultaneously, translation of the corpus Aristotelicum which introduces not only an alternative worldview but scientific (evidentialist discourse) as such. Before, the subordination of all other studies to the studies of the sacra pagina was not only uncontested but uncontestable. Now it becomes contestable and, progressively, will become contested.(Notice that there is no comparable rediscovery of, and interest in, ancient literature--this will wait until the Renaissance). With the two texts in theology and the influence of Aris, not only "questiones separatae a textu,"  but the "genre" of the quaestiones themselves begins to change.

By the XIII century the transition is consummated. Philosophy becomes clearly differentiated from theology, still subordinate though autonomous within limits, but conceivably independable and supreme or even unique. Theology becomes clearly speculative rather than storica . Stories by themselves can convey truth but are not themselves true [NOT PREDICATIVE, OR PREDICATIVE ONLY IN THE LITERAL SENSE?] (NOT STRICTLY INTELLIGIBLE, because neither necessary nor universal) and are, therefore, uninteresting unless cashed in statements. The religious reading of the Bible becomes devotional, relegated to preaching and personal meditation from which prayer arises, while the theological reading of the Bible in the schools becomes sientific (in terms of truth or falsity) and primarily theoretical (in terms of intellectual assent rather than of response).
