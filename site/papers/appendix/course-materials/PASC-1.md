PASCAL

TEXTS

- Chronology (from Blaise Pascal by Hugh M Davison).

- Blaise Pascal, Pensées , tr. Krailsheimer (Penguin Classics)

- Provincial Letter # 4 , (from The Essential Pascal , pp. 223- 238).

- The Memorial  ( Ibid ., pp. 205-206).

- A Conversation with Monsieur de Saci ( Ibid . pp. 328-346).

- Selected Thoughts.

- Questions for Discussion.

DIRECTIONS

- Read the Introduction to the Penguin Edition of the Pensées .

From it you will gain a general idea of Pascal's life and

and of the Pensées . (If questions or observations arise, note them to share them with the group).

- Read as much as possible from the Pensées . Make a few notes of the thoughts that strike you, to share your questions and comments with the group. - If you have little time, "Selected Thoughts" offers you a somewhat representative selection.

- The Conversation with Monsieur de Saci is supposed to give you some concrete background for the Pensées .

- Provincial Letter # 4 gives you a taste of Pascal's style (his wit, his irony, his intensity) in a lighter genre than

the Pensées .

- The short Memorial is Pascal's own expression of his mystical experience. Notice particularly the famous expression "God of Abraham, God of Isaac, God of Jacob, not of the philosophers and scientists.... God of Jesus Christ".

What does this expression suggest to you concerning the

relationship between faith and reason?

- "Questions for Discussion" are meant to help you to reflect on what you read and to organize your ideas in order to share them with the group.
