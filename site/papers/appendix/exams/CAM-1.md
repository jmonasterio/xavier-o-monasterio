Camus on the Problem of Violence

With few, if any, ethical problems did Camus struggle

more intensely than with the issue of the legitimacy of the use of violence. This paper follows his evolution in that regard.

Once Camus emerged from his initial period of absurdism in which all actions are equivalent (1937-1940), he took three successive stands concerning the moral legitimacy of violence:

(1) violence is sometimes justified (1941-1945), violence is always unjustifiable (1946-1947), violence is sometimes necessary but never justified ((1948-1960).

The last section of the paper is devoted to a critical appraisal of Camus' final stand.
