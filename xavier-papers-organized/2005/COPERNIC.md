                             COPERNICAN REVOLUTION

           "Copernican Revolution" is  preceded  by  my  notes  on  Georges
           Morel's, Problemes actuels de religion (Montaigne-Aubier,  Paris
           1968)


AVERTISSEMENT: "... le malaise concernant l'existence meme  de  Dieu.  C'est
sur ce malaise, dont les xtiens seraient candides de  croire  qu'il  ne  les
affecte pas, que nous avons centre nos preocupations. Sans doute...  il  est
contradictoire de poser le probleme de l'existence de Dieu  sans  mettre  en
jeu un systeme de representations. Mais  ici  encore  il  n'est  pas  besoin
d'une reference explicite au probleme de la divinite ou de  la  resurrection
du Xt pour introduire une critique creatrice de ces representations (p.  8).
MY COMMENT: Le  malaise  nous  affecte,  nous  les  xtiens.  Mais  ceci  est
equivoque. On n'a pas un probleme commun avec tous les  hommes,  si  par  la
nous entendons un probleme 'abstrait' sur Dieu, qui est univoque  et  commun
a tous parce que nous voulons 'le Sens.'C'est la mon malaise majeur avec  ce
livre qui a tant de bonnes choses. En fin d'analyse, c'est de la  theodicee,
ce qui presuppose la division medievale entre foi  et  raison.  Avec  toutes
les attenuation kantiennes introduites par Morel, le Dieu qu'il atteint  est
atteint par  la  raison,  c.a.d.  comme  resultat  d'une  demarche  purement
rationnelle.
            "... dans theologie, il y a logos, raison,  discours,   langage,
et ... le mot theos tombe dans le vide quand le  logos  n'est  pas  pris  au
serieux" (Morel, see below,  p.  37).  ("discutons  entre  hommes  de  bonne
volonte et sachons de quoi nous discutons" p. 43).
      "CE QU'UN PAYS DETERMINE  EST  POUR  DES  DIEUX  DETERMINES  VENUS  DE
L'ETRANGER, LE PAYS DE LA RAISON L'EST POUR DIEU EN GRAL, UNE CONTREE OU  IL
CESSE D'EXISTER" (Marx dans sa dissertation doctorale, cite p.  66).      MY
COMMENT: Bien dit, si a raison est  la  raison  grecque,  la  raison  qui  a
determine pour le medievaux le pays de la foi.
      Morel dit ceci en parlant pour lui-meme: "la foi en  Dieu  ne  saurait
etre une opinion, elle ne saurait s'etablir  sur  le  sable  evanescent  des
sentiments ou sur  la  roche  pourrie  des  propositions  autoritaires;  les
termes jurent: si Dieu existe, il ne peut etre que 'raison', et  l'acte  par
lequel l'homme reconnait son existence ne peut etre qu'un acte  raisonnable,
l'acte  raisonable  lui-meme"  (pp.  108-9.  !?).  "une   metaphysique   est
possible, mieux que possible: elle existe dans  tout  esprit  et  dans  tout
coeur humains qui ne sont pas atteints de la  maladie  dogmatique-sceptique"
(Eric Weil resumant une  these  kantienne,  dans  Problemes  Kantiens,  Vrin
1963, p. 54; cite dans  la  p.  114).  Un  malentendu  sur  les  preuves  de
l'existence de Dieu: "nous voudrions etre persuades de l'existence  de  Dieu
par des signes en eux-memes suffisants;nous voudrions  decouvrir  au  niveau
de la necessite phenomenale  ce  qui  par  essence  est  de  l'ordre  de  la
liberte. Mais... la libete n'est pas necessaire... elle est libre: c'est  la
precisement sa force et sa raison, sa verite - Un homme comme Kant nous  est
ici d'un remarquable example... Il s'agit pour nous de recevoir de  lui  une
grande lecon. On dit trop vite que la philo  de Kant aboutit au  fideisme...
Mais  on  ne  doit  pas  oublier  que  si  Kant  a  passe  au   crible   les
demonstrations de Dieu developpees  au  niveau  de  l'entendement,  c'est...
pour chercher a fonder au niveau fondamental, au niveau de la  liberte,  une
preuve eminemment rationnelle. Et cette preuve... il l'exprime...  au  terme
de la derniere Critique, mais... il l'exprime en filigrane, comme  si...  la
restriction etait de mise... La morale doit rester morale de  la  liberte...
l'idee de Dieu est une idee pratique, non le concept d'une  chose...  Il  ne
se montre pas a l'homme observateur, homme de  science,  homme  connaissant,
qui ne peut aller, sans subreption, au-dela de l'idee du  grand  horloger...
On comprend alors que Kant ait  tout  fait  pour  affaiblir  sa  preuve:  sa
force,  si  l'on  pouvait  la  juger  irresistible  selon  la   metaphysique
chosifiante, aurait ete  sa  defaite"  (p.  120-21.  En  partie,  c'est  une
citation de E. Weil, ope, cit. pp.  94-95).  "Homme  et  Sens,  c'est  d'eux
seuls qu'ils'agit dans l'histoire. Le Sens est pour l'homme et l'homme  hors
du Sens (c'est la tautologie) s'evanouit en  neant"  (p.  129  !?).  "Preuve
veut dire ici experience libre et raisonnable qui de soi ne laisse  rien  en
dehors d'elle" (130). MON COMMENTAIRE: Les categories de pensee de Morel  se
revelent kantiennes. Il croit qu'il y a un nouveau 'fondement' chez Kant  au
dela de la raison pure, au niveau de  la  raison  pratique  telle  que  Kant
l'entend et de la liberte du sujet telle que Kant l'entend. D'apres moi,  le
'fondement' est dans la pensee au niveau de l'attitude naturelle, au  niveau
de la vulnerabilite, l'exposition.
      "Cependant, en fait,  cette  foi  en  l'ultime  fondement  reste  fort
souvent vague, confuse, inconsistante. Elle se dissipe tres  vite  dans  les
regions de la brume. C'est que  l'evidence  ou  l'immediatete  fondamentales
n'ont rien a voir avec une evidence ou  une  immediatete  naturelles.  Elles
sont d'un  tout  autre  ordre.  Elles  demandent  justement  la  destruction
progressive  de  cette  double  attitude  naturelle  qui  s'imagine  pouvoir
reconnaitre le Sens, soit au niveau de la nature, soit a son propre  niveau.
L'homme ne  saurait  donc  eprouver  Dieu  comme  il  eprouve  les  realites
visibles, fussent-elles sacrees: l'homme n'est pas un animal. Il ne  saurait
non plus eprouver Dieu par l'abolition en lui de la finitude: l'homme  n'est
pas un Dieu. C'est pourquoi, dans  son  rapport  au  fondement  dernier,  la
raison est toujours raison dans l'inconnu et dans la nuit:  elle  n'est  pas
intuition, elle est foi. Foi raison" (p. 131). "Marx insiste  plus  sur  les
conditions de la liberte que sur la liberte" (p. 139).  "Nous  avons  appele
cette attitude fondamentale 'foi-raison':  expression  qui  par  sa  dualite
vise a eliminer en meme temps  les  fautes  du  fideisme,  inclin,  sous  le
pretexte de sauvegarder l'autonomie divine, a  voir  en  l'homme  un  animal
religieux inconscient de soi, et les fautes du rationalisme,  succombant  au
contraire a la tentation de reduire au meme niveau l'homme et  Dieu"  (140).
"'Les mots sont devenus dans les langues  humaines  ce  que  la  pensee  est
devenue dans l'esprit de l'homme. Ces mots  sont  devenus  comme  autant  de
morts qui enterrent des morts et qui souvent  meme  enterrent  des  vivants.
Ainsi l'homme s'enterre lui-meme avec  ses  propres  mots  alteres  qui  ont
perdu  tout  leur  sens.'  C'est  la  un  texte  remarquable.   Il   indique
parfaitement que si le langage deperit dans une civilisation, c'est  que  la
pensee y deperit, c'est enfin que l'homme y deperit. Ce texte ecrit  pendant
la Revolution francaise par celui  qui  signait  le  philosophe  inconnu..."
[Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin dans un ouvrage dont le  titre  est  a  noter:
Ecce Homo, paru en 1792] (p. 159).

Hegel, et encore moins Marx, dont Morel s'autorise  implicitement  quand  il
parle de liberte situee et enracinee dans le monde, n'ont rien a  voir  avec
la liberte kantienne et  la  pensee  grecque.  Ils  parlent  sur  une  autre
longeur d'onde.

      "De meme que les villes, lors d'un tremblement de terre,  s'effondrent
et retournent au desert, de meme que l'homme ne construit sa maison  sur  un
sol volcanique que furtivement et avec crainte, de meme la  vie  s'effondre,
faiblit et perd courage, quand le tremblement  des  concepts  cause  par  la
science ote a l'homme le fondement de sa securite et de son  repos,  la  foi
dans le durable et l'eternel" (NIETZSCHE, cite p. 196).
      "En verite, vous ne  pouviez  porter  de  meilleur  masque  que  votre
propre visage, hommes de ce temps. Qui donc  pourrait  vous  reconnaitre?  -
Couverts des signes du passe et barbouilles de nouveaux signes:  ainsi  vous
etes-vous caches de tous les augures!" (Nietzsche, Zaratustra:  Du  pays  de
la civilisation, cite p. 197).    "Il  (NIETZSCHE)  se  dresse  une  seconde
objection devant celui  qui  ose  regarder  l'admiration  comme  un  element
capital de la liberte humaine: cette objection, obsedante  a  toute  epoque,
prend sa source dans le sentiment tragique de la vie.  Sentiment  fonde:  la
vie est tragique. Alors par quelle naivete ou quel egoisme un individu peut-
il se livrer a l'admiration d'un dieu et de sa creation,  au  milieu  de  la
violence et la misere, qui sont le pain quotidien?... L'objection  est  tres
grave" (198). "Il est constitutif de  la  realite,  il  est  constitutif  de
Dieu,  que  l'homme  devienne  homme  dans  le  combat,  le   travail,   les
epreuves... il nous  est  impossible  de  par  ls  nature  meme  des  choses
d'acceder a une liberte, du reste toujours menacee, sans  passer  par  cette
lutte et par ce  negatif.  Dieu  lui-meme  ne  peut  faire  qu'il  en  aille
autrement et si dans ce propos nous croyons  decouvrir  un  obstacle  a  son
existence, c'est que nous continuons de nourrir au fond de nos  esprits  une
image magique de la Paternite divine. Par consequent parler de la  condition
humaine, c'est  en  parler  selon  la  stricte  signification  suivante:  le
negatif est element essentiel de l'histoire, bien qu'il ne soit pas en  lui-
meme le sens. C'est pourquoi nous n'avons ni a nous courber devant lui ni  a
le fuir, ni a nous rejouir de lui  ni  a  en  gemir,  mais  a  essayer  avec
patience de le transformer. C'est pourquoi  nous  n'avons  pas  non  plus  a
accuser Dieu de son existence, confondant ainsi violence et raison, sens  et
non-sens" (203-4). "Precisons  encore.  Puisque  la  recherche  de  Dieu--la
realisation d'une vie sensee--est identiquement la prise au  serieux  de  la
condition  humaine,  elle  est  par  la  meme  LA  PRISE  AU   SERIEUX   DES
CONDITIONS.... depuis surtout un siecle et demi, nous sommes amenes, par  le
developpement scientifique et par l'espoir de transformation qu'il  souleve,
a un  regard  plus  juste  et  plus  minutieux  des  elements  particuliers,
innombrables, complexes, ambigus qui constituent notre nature,  individuelle
et sociale" (205).     MON COMMENTAIRE: Il absout Dieu trop  facilement.  SA
SOLUTION AU PROBLEME DU MAL ne tient pas compte , comme dirait Camus, de  la
mort d'un enfant. Je continue a penser que sans le Christ il n'y  a  pas  de
reponse au probleme du mal.
       "... EN REALITE LE PROBLEME N'EST PAS FINALEMENT  DE  LA  POSSIBILITE
MAIS DE LA NATURE DE CETTE CONNAISSANCE, puisque l'essence  de  l'homme  [JE
DIRAIS PLUTOT  L'ESSENCE  DE  LA  CONNAISSANCE]  est  reconnaissance,  c'est

se sacrifier.' Dans ce texte il est remarquable que LES DEUX  SENS--NOETIQUE
ET ACTIF--DU MEME MOT  SAVOIR  (WISSEN)  sont  immediatement  conjoints:  LE
SAVOIR SANS ACTION EST STERILE, COMME L'ACTION  DEPOURVUE  DE  SAVOIR  TOMBE
DANS L'INCOHERENCE" (210). "Cette certitude ne peut donc pas  se  demontrer,
non pas qu'elle soit  irrationnelle,  mais  parce  que  c'est  en  elle  que
s'accomplit la raison" (211).
       "Or l'ineffabilite dont il est ici question n'a rien a voir  avec  la
fuite illusoire a l'interieur de l'infini: elle  signifie  au  contraire  LE

FINIE... C'EST POURQUOI L'EXPERIENCE EST  TOUJOURS  EXPERIENCE  DE  LA  NON-
EXPERIENCE..... Bref, si je me tais  alors,  ce  n'est  pas  par  defaut  du

DEFINITIVEMENT AU RESEAUX DU LANGAGE: ELLE Y PASSE MAIS N'Y  DEMEURE  PAS...
LE  NIVEAU  LINGUSTIQUE  N'EST  PAS  LE  NIVEAU  LE  PLUS   FONDAMENTAL   DE
L'EXPERIENCE.  LE  LANGAGE  SE  REALISE-T-IL  EN  INVITANT  LUI-MEME  A  SON

Comme l'ecrit Derrida, 'la paix comme le silence  est  la  vocation  etrange

propre,determine, fini. Se revelant, Dieu  me  revele  a  moi,immediatement.
C'est pour moi que  Dieu  est  l'Autre.  Non  pas  le  Tout-Autre,  l'absolu
Etranger avec qui je n'aurais aucun  rapport..."  (216).  "De  toute  facon,
l'idee eschatologique vise par definition  la  fin--l'accomplissement--d'une
histoire et exige par consequent cette histoire. Une  histoire  ou  la  nuit
est nuit, et non pas jour, ni meme seulement l'ombre amicale du jour. Il  ne
s'agit pas de considerer l'inconnu qui s'etend devant  nous  comme  un  faux
inconnu, de sorte que l'histoire serait le deroulement  theatral  d'un  plan
preprae a l'avance. ON COMPREND  MIEUX  AUSSI  POURQUOI  CE  N'EST  PAS  PAR
SOUMISSION SERVILE QUE L'ON REFUSE DE DRESSER A DIEU UN PROCES AU  SUJET  DU
MAL QUI SEVIT DANS LE MONDE, comme si justement le sens etait deja  realite.
A  supposer  que  l'histoire  comme  histoire,  c.  a.  d  comme  'totalite'
accomplie, soit pensable, il est  evident  que  ce  n'est  pas  aujourd'hui,
puisque  cette  totalite  n'existe  pas.  Ainsi  l'histoire   est   vraiment
constitutive de l'homme. Comme l'ecrit Eric  Weil  dans  sa  Logique  de  la
Philo [p. 415], 'la philo s'est emparee de l'histoire non comme  d'un  objet
exterieur et resistant, mais comme de sa propre  essence,  qui  ainsi  cesse
d'etre  essence  seulement,  d'etre  fond  insaisissable  et   qui   echappe
toujours: l'histoire est la philo en devenir, LA PHILO  EXPRIME  LA  REALITE
DE L'HOMME  A  REALISER  DANS  L'HISTOIRE.'  Disons,  pour  eviter...  toute
equivoque: l'histoire est l'humanite en devenir.  C'EST  POUR  CETTE  RAISON
QU'IL FAUT SE MEFIER DE LA TENDANCE  DES  RELIGIONS  POSITIVES  A  CHOSIFIER
L'HISTOIRE, COMME SI ON POUVAIT  Y  DECOUVRIR  UNE  PREUVE  TANGIBLE  DE  LA
DIVINITE" (218-219). "Mais l'homme n'est rien sans le corps, sans  l'action,

                             COPERNICAN REVOLUTION
                                  As all seeing is seeing-as, so
                                  all living is living-as, however
                                  unreflective it may be.
LA REVOLUTION COPERNICIENNE DES GRECS
      Kant thought he had effected a philosophical  revolution  by  shifting
the weight from the object to the subject. In fact,  he  was  only  creating
another figure within the Greek paradigm within which  "philosophy,"  as  we
understand it, was born. There has been only one copernican  revolution--the
one effected by the Greeks: from  the  innocent  attitude  to  the  critical
attitude.
      Insofar as it was a phenomenon of growth, a passage from childhood  to
adolescence, the Greek  revolution  was  inevitable,  given  time.  Insofar,
however, as it involved an escape  from  the  natural  attitude,  the  Greek
Copernican revolution was a tragic mistake from whose  consequences  we  are
still suffering. For we are and remain the children of the Greeks.
      There is a sense in which we, Westerners, will always remain  children
of the Greeks, for history cannot be undone. Nor would it even be  desirable
to undo that tremendously  significant  episode  of  our  history,  for  the
critical attitude is a progress over the innocence  of  childhood.  In  this
regard--but only in this regard--I agree with Husserl that there is a  sense
in which no one would  prefer  to  'indianize  himself'  (Die  Krisis).  But
Husserl also meant what he said as if the Greeks, through their  revolution,
had created the genuine paradigm of rationality, as  if  the  Greek  passage
from the innocent to the critical attitude had actually coincided  with  the
passage from irrationality to rationality. On  this,  I  disagree.  In  this
Husserl remained, alas, a child of Kant  and  of  his  acknowledged  father,
Descartes, and through them a child of  Plato--in  short,  a  child  of  the
Greeks.
      The Greek  revolution  involved  an  identification  of  the  critical
attitude with the unnatural attitude of "reason,"  that  is,  of  the  pure,
unexposed contemplator, of the unidirectional  presence  of  the  phenomenal
object to the phenomenal subject, with the implicit, inescapable  separation
between the noumenal and the phaenomenal. The genuine Copernican  revolution
to be effected under the pressure of the present 'crisis  of  reason,'  that
is, of Greak "reason," is the passage from  the  innocent  attitude  to  the
critical attitude within the genuinely natural attitude.  (I  speak  of  the
genuinely natural attitude by contrast to the mistaken way in which  Husserl
understood the natural attitude).
      What is the genuinely natural attitude? The attitude  of  the  exposed
subject in bi-directional and effective (not only intentional)  presence  to
the surrounding world.
      Why is this attitude to be preserved? Because it  happens  to  be  our
inescapable,  effective  mode  of  insertion  in  the  world  and  our   the
foundation  of  our  intentional  opening  to  the  world  and  because,  if
neutralized and unacknowledged in its factual legitimacy--as  it  is  within
the Greek paradigm--it necessarily gives  origin  to  our  present  lack  of
reflective or critical self-understanding of  ourselves  and  our  world  as
well as to the unsolvable quandaries  or  aporiae  in  which  we  have  been
involved since the resurrection of the  Greek  "reason"  in  the  thirteenth
century.
      Among these aporiae, the ones that  affect  the  Christian  faith  are
particularly important to me as a Christian, for they create  an  unbearable
tension between my faith and my life as an intellectual who functions  under
the strictures of the Greak "reason," and force me into a sort of bad  faith
in my life as a faithful for lack of the appropriate  categories  for  self-
understanding. As an intellectual, I  am  a  child  of  the  Greek  critical
reason. As a Christian, I am a child of the Hebrew innocent  but  legitimate
natural attitude. As an intellectual who is a child  of  the  Greeks,  I  am
forced to judge my faith by the  standards  of  Greek  reason  to  which  it
neither can nor  ought  to  live  up,  but  which  make  me  find  my  faith
defective. Thus, as a  Christian  intellectual,  I  am  projected  into  the
corner of a fideism in bad faith, because my  only  choice  is  between  the
critical attitude identified  with  the  "reason"  of  the  Greeks  and  the
innocent attitude of  my  Hebrew  heritage  of  faith  identified  with  the
uncritical attitude. On the other hand, as a a Christian whose faith is  the
standard by which everything is judged, I am projected in  my  discourse  to
other intellectuals into the corner of a suspect intellectual  who  attempts
the impossible task of imposing to (Greek) rationality  the  limits  of  his
faith and of balancing two all-embracing, incompatible paradigms.
      The issue, however, is not limited to the field  of  faith.  Believers
and non-believers, we all are swept together today by the crisis of  (Greek)
reason. It is not only as believers but as humans that we need a way out  of
the Greek paradigm of rationality into a paradigm which is not a  regression
from the critical attitude to the innocent attitude, but rather  a  critical
reconquest of the natural  attitude.  Such  regression  would  not  only  be
undesirable; it is both  unacceptable  and  impossible.  I  think  that  the
critical return to the genuine natural attitude is the way out,  the  needed
Copernican revolution. From it I do not expect anything  more,  but  nothing
less  either,  than  a  common,  acceptable  paradigm   within   which   our
differences become intelligible and respectable. As G. Morel writes,  "...il
n'y a pas de confrontation possible avec  l'atheisme,  sans  acceptation  de
dialogue avec des athees, c.a.d. sans une attitude qui  ne  soit  ni  simple
refus  ni  conciliation  equivoque"  (George  Morel,  Problemes  actuels  de
religion, Aubier-Montaigne, Paris 1968, p. 34. SEE 'ETUDES", 1964-67).
CHRISTIAN CRITICAL THOUGHT
      There is no passage to adulthood but from  childhood,  from  a  given,
determined childhood. The greeks moved to critical thought  from  their  own
given, determined childhood which defined their  problems.  What  about  us,
Christians?  Historically,  our  Christian  childhood   emerged   from   the
childhood of Judaism. The rupture-continuity effected by Christ  gave  birth
to a new, original mode of life. It was by comparison to "the Law",  because
there was a felt dissonance between the new mode of life  and  the  mode  of
life of the Law,  that  Christians  became  little  by  little  reflectively
conscious of their  Christian  identity.  A  similar,  less  systematic  and
easier work of comparison with the pagans and with the pagan religions  took
place  and  contributed  to  clarify  for  the  Christians  their  Christian
identity. This task of reflective self-identification, however,  took  place
on  the  reflective  but  uncritical  level.  The  real  passage  from   the
uncritical to the critical level occurred in the thirteenth century  on  the
occasion  of  the  irruption  of  Aristotle's  works.  Till  then  Christian
thinkers had used certain tools of the Greek critical reason,  such  as  the
logic and some scattered elements of the Aristotelian epistemology,  but  as
mere tools which THEY  AS  CHRISTIANS  had  used.  The  structure  of  their
Weltanschaung however had remained historical,  in  rupture-continuity  with
the Jewish Weltanschaung. The Fathers thought in terms of "the  Economy,"--a
history, the one and only History, Sacred History" within  which  their  own
lives and everybody's life took place and was meant to find its place.  From
the time of  their  encounter  with  Aristotle's  works  on  things  changed
completely and irreversibly. As thinkers, the Greeks had  moved  from  their
Weltanschaung to the Kingdom of (Greek) reason. It was,  therefore,  in  the
encounter between Christianity and Aristotelianism, not only a matter of  an
unreconcilable   tension   between   two   different    and    all-embracing
Weltanschaungen--after all, Weltanschaungen are, by  definition,  different,
all-embracing and therefore mutually exclusive--but a  tension  between  two
heteroclite universes of discourse: the discourse of and within the  natural
attitude and  the  discourse  of--but  not  within,  because  the  unnatural
attitude  is  uninhabitable,  because  it  is  a  'system'  rather  than   a
Weltanschaung--the unnatural attitude of the Greeks.
      What was  so  new  in  the  encounter  with  Aristotle?  Why  did  the
encounter with Aristotle produce such a cataclism whose waves still  shatter
us? Because it involved at the same  time  and  in  the  same  movement  the
passage from the innocent to the critical level and from a Weltanschaung  to
a closed and  all-embracing  explanatory  system.  Those  who  effected  the
passage did not realize the radical  difference  between  Weltanschaung  and
system. They assumed the Greek reason whose correlate is a "system,"  within
which there is no room for Weltanschaungen. Ultimately,  and  we  are  still
there, they condemned  their  successors  to  incoherence.  Ironically,  the
Greek  reason  which  was  in  Greece  a  transitory  phenomenon  that  soon
dissolved  itself  into  scepticism,  which  is  its  natural   consequence,
prospered in the Western world thanks to  its  articulation  into  the  very
Christian Weltanschaung that it was bound to corrode from  the  inside.  The
very body the microbe was bound to  kill  was  to  secrete,  thanks  to  the
transformation suffered by the body  because  of  the  implantation  of  the
microbe, the form of 'systematic' discourse that  would  defend  and  foster
the development of the microbe.
      [To be fair and make full sense, it is necessary to give  its  due  to
the Greek reason concering the development of the  sciences  in  the  modern
sense of the word].
      To fully explain myself, it is essential  to  render  perspicuous  and
persuasive  the  difference  between  system  and  Weltanschaung   and   the
confusion between the two consecrated in the thirteenth  century.  In  other
words,  "the  irruption"  of  Aristotelianism  needs  detailed,   convincing
explanation. What is  a  system?  What  is  a  Weltanschaung?  What  is  the
difference between the two?

For the moment, in a merely intuitive way and to fertilize the soil, I  will
comment briefly on the quotations  concerning  Nietzsche  and  Hegel  in  my
notes on Morel.
      NIETZSCHE (pp. 169-170 de Morel). - Nietzsche, as many  others,  feels
the weight of Greek reason on himself and on our culture  and  he  tries  to
escape from it. As usual, he is sharp in the destructive task. His  analysis
cum criticism  of  the  all-embracing  and  systematic  character  of  Greek
knowing reason, of the contemplative ideal, and of the insecurity  by  which
it is inspired is too general but right. Curiously, he  remains  subservient
to the Greeks  in  his  recognition  of  the  inability  to  "penetrate  the
phaenomena." This task is only conceivable within the space of Greek  reason
inherited by Kant, to which  Nietzsche  offers  no  viable  alternative.  In
fact, however, the point is not to reach the  noumenon  by  penetrating  the
phaenomena--for the split between phaenomenon and noumenon  is  Greek--  but
to acknowledge the temporal, contigent opening  of  future  history  already
indicated by the 'beyond' of situated perception and exposure.
      HEGEL (p. 235 de Morel).  -  The  idea  of  the  inter-relational  and
historical subject was introduced by  Christianity  (by  contrast  to  Greek
thought, ibidem above), and it has not been taken seriously yet. Much  truth
here, but perhaps not quite in the Hegelian sense. As  indicated  by  Morel,
there is something wrong in the discourse of the Phenomenolgy that  embraces
both the subject and God. There is therefore, it seems, on Hegel's  part,  a
recuperation of history by system. History itself is systematic. There is  a
thinkable totality in terms of teleology. If this is  what  Hegel  means  by
taking seriously the idea of the inter-relational  and  historical  subject,
this is not the  idea  introduced  by  Christianity,  and  it  is  not  what
Christianity introduced. The idea of  the  inter-relational  and  historical
subject does not necessitate Christianity and does not include  the  God  of
Christianity. In the last analysis, any  reflective,  critical  analysis  of
one's own Weltanschaung, any 'self-mediation by the  system'  (to  speak  in
Hegel's terms) yields the idea of the inter-relational, historical  subject.
The  atheistic  existentialists  could  have  reached  that   idea   without
Christianity and without Hegel, just by escaping from Greek thought,  though
historically they may have reached that idea by deteleologizing Hegel or  by
detheologizing the Christian Weltanschaung. The Christian  Weltanschaung  is
not teleological in the sense of implying a history that is teleological  in
itself and whose teleology could be discovered by a Hegelian  self-mediation
by the system on the part of the subject involved in  that  history  (or  of
the subject of history). The Christian  Weltanschaung  is  not  teleological
even in the sense of implying that God  revealed  to  us  the  teleology  of
history and that it is accessible  through  faith  to  the  Christians.  For
Christian revelation is not revelation of a system, not even of  a  Hegelian
system of return to the Absolute. Christianity  is  totally  and  thoroughly
historical, contingent and  gratuitous  and  surprising  before,  with,  and
after Christ. It is not as if after Christ and thanks to Him  and  in  faith
we Christians have the right Hegelian philosophy, though the philosophy  can
be accepted only by those who have the faith.  It  is  that  wuat  would  be
comparable to a philosophy remains mysterious, as fulgurant  and  as  opaque
as Christ himself. We Christians replicate the  mystery  of  Christ  in  our
little  way.  Like  Him  we  consent  and  will  to  go  God's  way  without
explanation  on  His  part.  For  in  Christ   God   revealed   Himself   by
participating with us in history; He did not explain Himself.  This  is  why
we Christians "discern."
      But what is this discernment? Does not Christianity, then,  amount  to
a deteleologized Hegelianism, that is, to an illusory variety  of  atheistic
existentialism? If there is no self-mediation  by  Christ  (instead  of  the
system), then there is no Christian discernment. If that were the case,  the
life, death and resurrection of Christ would have ceased  to  be  revelatory
and become brute events, however effective. If that  were  the  case,  there
would be more courage and  truth  in  the  discernment  that  the  atheistic
existentialist practices by inventing his or her ends. Here I have  returned
to the very 'rational' illusion I am trying to transcend. I want  to  define
and distinguish and justify Christian discernment in  such  a  way  that  it
becomes specifically different from all other discernment  and,  ultimately,
that the discernment itself predefines  the  form  of  the  content.  Fausse
piste! There is no foundational discourse, not  even  within  faith.  Either
faith is 'home' in faith, or it is nothing. Hope is the  faithful  teleology
of  faith. Hope gives  us  the  transtemporal  closure  of  history  without
giving us any temporal closure. We advance in  faith  and  hope,  living  in
charity and moving onto and toward death--onto and toward resurrection.
