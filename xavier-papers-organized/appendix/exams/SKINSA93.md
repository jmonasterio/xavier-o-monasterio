                                        NAME____________________

1)  Skinner  claims  that  his  "operant  conditioning,"   unlike   Watson's
"classical behaviorism," has truly nothing to do with consciousness or  "the
inner man."
      (a) In what way did classical behaviorism include consciousness?
      (b) Is it true that  operant  conditioning  has  nothing  to  do  with
consciousness? Explain why, or why not.

2) Would Skinner be able to tell the difference  between  lying  and  saying
something false by mistake? If so, how? If not, why not?

3) Would Sartre say that the Epsilons who do not revolt are  in  bad  faith?
Explain why, or why not.

4) By "People are not what they are" Sartre means that if you are a  student
you really are not a student.
      Is the above statement correct? Explain why, or why not.

5) Who  of  the  two--Sartre  or  Skinner--would  bring  rapists  to  trial?
Explain.
