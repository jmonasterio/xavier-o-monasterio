\wp\97\97-hints Dec. 26, 97

HINTS

- Everybody, except Aquinas and perhaps Bonaventure, was anti-Aristotelian in the controversy whether theology was a science.

- The issue of the rationality of faith, with the result that

the commitment of faith amounted to fideism , unless supported by the praeambula fidei. This rationalism was only partially redressed by Vatican II, and not openly, so that the intellectualistic-rationalistic distortion prevalent in the Catholic intellectual tradition was not really redressed. (Delhougne).

- No room for intentional emotions and attitudes. They become blind, Humean feelings that arbitrarily accompany certain representations.

- The truth is one. When truth is understood as contemplative correspondence, then unavoidable conflict between the theologians' and the physicists' pictures. Then teaching the right picture in the schools through 'proven' doctrines, and defending 'the rights of the truth' in the city (when Christians constitute the majority, or becoming relativists and losing the sense of evangelizing when Christians live in a pluralistic, tolerant society) become the rule.   

- The equivocal status of the practical in Aristotle and throughout the philosophic al tradition. Aristotle speaks of the practical intellect and of practical knowledge , whereas there is no possible truth in the realm of the practical.
