2004-abstraction

                                      ABSTRACTION AND APPLICATION

There is in the Aristotelian tradition the famous doctrine of the three kinds of abstraction in science, which is a reflective activity. Abstraction refers to ‘object’ of knowledge, to that which is supposed to correspond to the real object in the knower’s mind. But it refers also to the object of predication to which the categories apply, which is the real object. At any rate, it never refers to what is encountered by the situated agent, which is, in my opinion, what scientific language refers the agent to and which comes to fruition at the moment of application. There is, however, an equivocation in the way in which the word ‘refers’ is used in the preceding sentences. When applied to the object encountered by the situated agent the reference is tacit and instrumental, whereas in the case of science and of predication the reference is objective and explicit.
