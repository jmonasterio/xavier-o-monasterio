2004-June-2-project           June 5, 2004

PROJECT NUSSBAUM

Striking differences between Nussbaum and Devereux:

- Texts referred to

- Requirement of agent’s desire of a goal

          N.B. Desire, unlike thought and judgment, is non-propositional; hence TACIT.

- Availability of means in the agent’s concrete situation

- Explanation of the connection of knowledge with action:

Devereux:  - Indirect (through theoretical understanding)

      - Accidental (may or may not take place)

Direct:        - The knowledge itself terminates in action

      - Essential: If it does not terminate in action, it is not practical knowledge
