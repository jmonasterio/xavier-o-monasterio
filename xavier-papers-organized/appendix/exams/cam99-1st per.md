Cam99-1st per NAME________________

1) According to Camus, absurdity always involves a contradiction or disproportion between two terms. How does this apply to the case of the absurdity of life? Which are, in this particular case, the two terms, and wherein lies the disproportion or contradiction between them?

2) Camus argues that we should not commit suicide, given that life is absurd/ Why not?

3) Authenticity consists in acting according to one’s convictions. Is that the same as acting according to one’s preferences?

4)  Assume that Meursault, after killing the Arab, had behaved as if he repented and had been set free. Would Camus have found that way of playing the game incoherent with his own philosophy? Explain.

5)  Why does Camus consider Meursault more admirable than a dog that eats, copulates, and sleeps whenever it feels like doing that? And do you agree with Camus on this point?
