                                        MY PROBLEM

      If philosophy in general, both in its practice and  in  its  teaching,
is marked by Greek categories, even more so is philosophy of religion.  Yet,
Pascal's objection remains. The God of philosophers is not the God of Jesus-
Christ. Escaping the God of philosophers is no great deed.  Every  Christian
does it everyday. Escaping the God of philosophers as  an  intellectual,  in
good faith, is no little deed.  Escaping that God as a philosopher and as  a
teacher of philosophy is well neigh to impossible, because of the weight  of
the philosophical tradition. The tradition is not  only  Greek;  it  is  the
Christian intellectual tradition inaugurated by the medievals who  submitted
their statements of faith to the strictures  of  the  Greek  categories  and
consecrated those categories.
      Clearly, the God of Christianity is primarily historical. And this  in
several senses. A God who  freely,  gratuitously,  graciously  irrupts  into
history. A God who  cannot  be  either  deduced  or  induced.  A  God  whose
intelligibility  is  historically  presented  in  the  Bible.  A   God   who
transforms the history  in  the  making  to  which  we  belong.  There  are,
possibly, other senses in which our God is historical.  And  none  of  these
senses can figure as such and without radical distortion  within  the  space
of the Greek categories, which themselves can be understood  only  in  terms
of the critical rational project of the Greeks by which the  medievals  were
inspired.
