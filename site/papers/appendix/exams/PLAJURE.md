(1) If Camus, in The Plague , had judged Grand by the same standard he applies to Rieux, Rambert, Tarrou, and Cottard, would he have said that Grand won the match? Explain why, or why not.

(2) Imagine that Camus were hiding a Jew in Nazi Germany. Given his position in The Rebel concerning the justification of the means by the end, would Camus lie to the Nazis to save the life of the Jew? If yes, how does Camus position differ from Monasterio's? If not, how does Camus position differ from Tarrou's?

(3) What does Camus mean in the Introduction to The Rebel by "We are living in the era of premeditation and the perfect crime"? Why "premeditation"? Why "perfect crime"?

(4) Stepan's position concerning the justification of the means by the end is basically the same position Camus held in his first period.

Is the above statement correct? Explain why, or why not.

(5) Comment on the two most important insights you gained through this course.
