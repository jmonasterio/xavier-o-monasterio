- p. 30: The conflict is not about priests and sacrifice... Rather, his protest was against a domination system legitimated in the name of God.

- p. 66: Within Judaism, the belief in a life after death was a relatively recent development. It emerged two centuries earlier with the martyrdom of faithful Jews who resisted the Hellenistic emperor Antiochus Epiphanes IV. Its purpose was to redress human injustice. Jews who were faithful to God were being executed, and collaborators were beign spared. Thus belief in resurrection was a way of defending God’s justice : the martyrs would receive blessed afterlife. By the time of Jesus, the majority of Jews affirmed life after death. So apparently did Jesus, even though life after death was not the focus of his message.
