a - John Monfasani, "Humanism and Rhetoric" (vol. III, pp. 171-235). THE VERY LONG NOTES CONTAIN ABUNDANT BIBLIOGRAPHY.

"SO THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN MEDIEVAL AND RENAISSANCE RHETORIC HAD NOTHING ESSENTIAL TO DO WITH THE AMOUNT OF CLASSICAL RHETORIC AVAILABLE. RAHTER, THE DIFFERENCE LAY IN THE VIEW EACH PERIOD TOOK OF THE USE AND VALUE OF RHETORIC. fEUDAL SOCIETY HAD LITTLE SYMPATHY FOR THE SECULAR ORATORICAL CULTURE OF ANTIQUITY AND, THEREFORE, LIMITED NEED OF THE ART THAT HAD SERVED THAT ORATORICAL CULTURE AND HAD BEEN A A FUNDAMENTAL PART OF CLASSICAL EDUCATION. iN THE MIDDLE AGES RHETORIC SUFFERED A SEVERE DEMOTION. iT LOST ITS EDUCATIONAL PREEMINENCE FIRST TO GRAMMAR AND THEN, FROM THE THIRTEENTH CENTURY ON, TO LOGIC ..." (P. 173)

"BY CONTRAST, THE MEDIEVAL DISMEMBERMENT OF THE CLASSICAL ART TO MEET CONTEMPORARY NEEDS WAS A BRILLIANT SUCCESS. THIS FRAGMENTATION TOOK TWO GUISES. ONE WAS TO USE CLASSICAL RHETORIC FOR ESSENTIALLY NONRHETORICAL PRUPOSES. I HAVE ALREADY MENTIONED THE PSEUDO-ISOCRATEAN AD DEMONICUM SERVING AS A TEXT IN ETHICS. THE SAME HAPPENED TO ARISTOTLE'S RHETORIC . MEDIEVAL SCHOLARS ALSO FOUND ARISTOTLE'S RHETORIC USEFUL AS A TEXT IN PSYCHOLOGY, SINCE IT IS THE ONLY PLACE IN WHICH ARISTOTLE DISCUSSES THE EMOTIONS. IN ANTIQUITY, RHETORICAL INVENTION AND DIALECTIS SHARED THE ARGUMENTATIVE TECHNIQUE OF THE TOPOI . BUT IN THE THIRTEENTH CENTURY, AS CLASSICAL RHETORIC FELL BY THE WAYSIDE, THE NEW LOGICAL MANUALS TOO OVER EXCLUSIVE INSTRUCTION IN THE TOPOI . WHEN TOPICS WERE TAUGHT, THEY WERE TAUGHT AS A PART OF LOGIC, NOT  RHETORIC. IN FACT, THE MIDDLE AGES ESPECIALLY CONCEIVED OF THE CLASSICAL ART IN TERMS OF STYLE, VIEWING RHETORIC AS A SOURCE OF PROSE AND VERSE COLORES AND EXORNATIONES ... THE INITIAL GOAL OF THE HUMANISTS WAS TO PUT ALL THE PIECES TOGETHER AGAIN" 9PP. 173-4). "BUT BY AND LARGE ITALIAN HUMANISTS RESPECTED THE DIFFERENCE BETEEN THE TWO DISCIPLINES AND LEFT LOGIC TO THE SCHOLASTICS. - NORTHERN HUMANISM TOOK A DIFFERENT TACK... IN AGRICOLA'S MIND THE PARADIGMATIC ORATOR WAS A TEACHER, A MAGISTER, AND NOT SPECIFICALLY A POLITICAL LEADER, A DUX POPULI. THE RESULT WAS A PECULIARLY NORTHERN APPROACH TO ORATORY. - ... BUT WHAT REALLY MATTERED IS THAT HE [AGRICOLA] STOOD ON ITS HEAD THE CLASSICAL DICTUM THAT ORATORS MUST MOVE, PLEASE, AND TEACH. LORENZO VALLA HAD ARGUED FROM THIS DICTUM TO THE VIEW THAT RHETORIC ENCOMPASSED LOGIC, SINCE THE LOGICIAN MERELY TAUGHT WHILE THE ORATOR DID THE SAME AND MUCH MORE. AGRICOLA, HOWEVER, APPARENTLY FOLLOWING ST AUGUSTINE'S THE DOCTRINA CHRISTIANA (4.27-28), INSISTED THAT TEACHING WAS THE FUNDAMENTAL TASK OF THE SPEAKER AND THAT THE OFFICIA OF MOVING AND PLEASING WERE SUBSIDIARY. THUS, IN AGRICOLA'S WORK, LOGIC, WHICH HAD THE SPECIFIC TASK OF TEACHING, RETAINED PRIMACY OVER RHETORIC... BUT THE DECISIVE FACTOR IN AGRICOLA'S SCHEME WAS HIS EQUATION OF DIALECTICAL WITH RHETORICAL TOPOI . SINCE THESE TOPOI WERE AT ROOT THE SAME, LOGIC, NOT RHETORIC, BEST TREATED THEM... IN OTHER WORDS, RHETORIC DIFFERED FROM LOGIC MERELY IN THAT IT PRESENTED MORE PRETELY AND LOOSELY WHAT WLOGIC HAD DEMONSTRATED IN STRICT FASHION. AGRICOLA LEFT NO DOUBT ABOUT THIS INTERPRETATION WHEN DISCUSSISNG DELECTATIO, ONE OF THE OFFICIA OF THE ORATOR. TRUE, HE ADMITTED, DELECTATION WAS THE TERRAIN OF THE ORATOR, BUT THE PLEASURE OF A SPEECH DERIVED BOTH FROM ITS CONTENT ( RES ), WHICH APPEALS TO THE MIND, AND FROM ITS RHETORICAL ACCOUTREMENTS, WHICH APPEAL TO THE SENSES. THE FORMER, THE SUBSTANCE OF THE SPEECH, WAS THE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE DIALECTICUS. - AGRICOLA HAD REHORICIZED LOGIC; BUT HE HAD ALSO DEVALUED RHETORIC, MAKING IT AN APPENDAGE OF LOGIC. HIS IDEAL FOUND LITTLE FAVOR IN ITALY" (PP. 195-7).
