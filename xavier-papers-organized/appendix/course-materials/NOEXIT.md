\WP\a\NOEXIT                            MIRRORS IN NO EXIT

Skeets
- Absence of mirrors forces to trust the perception of others
- Hell is other people. Perhaps if  there  had  been  mirrors  around,  this
would not have been so true.  (?)

John
- In the mirror I become  object  for  myself,  but  my  subjectivity  still
escapes me because I am my own subjectivity.
- A mirror represents  the  possibility  to  recapture  one's  transcendence
which the Other has presently transcended. The mirror provides one  with  an
opportunity to pass judgment  upon  oneself.  It  seems,  though,  that  the
damage has already been done.

Louise
- Because of the absence of mirrors, the characters have  to  rely  on  each
other to reflect upon themselves. We get our images of  ourselves  from  the
conceptions that others make of us. A mirror really tells us nothing.
- It is important to Garcin to know what they think  of  him.  Yet,  at  the
same time, he set his own projects in life, and has to  take  responsibility
for them.

Leslie
- Estelle required the presence of others in order to  be  or  at  the  very
least she required the proof of her existence from a mirror.
- But Estelle becomes trapped in the person that Inez (whose  tastes  differ
from hers) sees in her.

Anne
- Through their dependence on each other, they become  objects--their  locus
of self stolen.
- Without reflection, we do not see or know ourselves. But even with it,  we
see ourselves from our own point of view. If the other did not  exist,  part
of our existence would not be.
- Reveals the dangers of our dependence on others  as  constructors  of  our
selves.

Jeff
- That the experience of being object to  another  human  is  a  fundamental
experience of the individual. Being in  the  presence  of  others  helps  to
define the human being.
- The lack of mirrors removes any way  for  the  characters  to  objectively
examine their appearance.

Andy
-  Estelle's need of a mirror to be sure that  she  exists  reminded  me  of
Sartre's point that that it is not that we have a body but  that  we  are  a
body. Inez responds that she is always conscious of  herself  in  her  mind.
Sartre  seems  to  be  making  the  suggestion  that   our   existence   and
subjectivity go beyond our bodies as well.

David
- A mirror allows the for-itself to view the body as object, and not  merely
experience the body as subject. This is exactly the  problem  with  mirrors.
They allow us to see ourselves as an object, but not  in  the  same  way  as
other people see us.

Tony
- The desperation with which they seek their reflection seems to  stem  from
the need of their self-affirmation.
- The characters cannot face themselves. They have blocked the  deeds  done.
The form of hell that the characters have come upon  is  one  in  which  the
other characters act as mirrors. They have the ability to  see  into  others
but not into themselves.

Jennifer
-  Their  lack  of  self-reliance  forces  them  to  rely  on   others   for
affirmation.
- If Sartre believes that I am no longer master of  the  situation  when  my
experience is transformed  in  front  of  the  other,  then  how  can  I  be
completely free.

Kathryn
- Estelle makes the statement that if she can't see herself, she  begins  to
doubt her own existence. For her, this is probably more true  than  for  the
others. Estelle entire existence dependens on her beauty, somthing  that  is
completely other-related.
- When the door opens they do  not  leave.  If  they  leave,  they  will  be
entirely alone. Their world would become a dream world.
