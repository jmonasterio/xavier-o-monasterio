1) The truth to which the questions refers is the truth of the absurdity of life, which Caligula thinks makes happiness impossible. The very fact that in the novel Camus judges people by their ability to strike the right balance between duty and happiness, and that he thinks that Rambert won  the match, shows that in his second period Camus disagrees with Caligula.

2) Inauthenticity is the disparity between convictions and actions. Caligula thinks that Cherea shares with him the same philosophical convictions but has not the courage to live accordingly.

Caligula is clearly wrong: Cherea agrees with Caligula that life is absurd, but he doesn’t think that all actions are on equal footing and everything is a matter of preferences.

3) Philosophical suicide consists in abandoning rational thought and leaping into belief in God.

Don Emmanuel may induce his people to commit philosophical suicide in order to protect their happiness, but he himself doesn’t believe in God. So he doesn’t commit philosophical suicide.

4) Existentialism in the strict sense –that is, the philosophy popularized by Camus and Sartre in the early Forties– is incompatible with Christianity, since it denies the existence of God and of moral obligations. But in the broad sense –as a style of philosophizing that differs from traditional philosophy– it is compatible with Christianity. That is why in his lecture on Existentialism Sartre granted that there are Existentialists who are Christian, though he didn’t explain how that was possible.

5) Winning and losing the match amounts to striking, or failing to strike, the right balance between one’s happiness and one’s duty. Both Rieux and Cottard lost the match, but for different reasons: Rieux because he counted overmuch on time, Cottard because he cared only for his own happiness with no consideration at all for the happiness of others. Whether Camus would have found worse the way in which Rieux or Tarrou lost the match can be argued either way, since Camus thought that one’s own happiness was no less important than the happiness of others.
