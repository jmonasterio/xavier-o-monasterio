September 26, 2000

Robert Stack

247 Valley Road

Princeton, New Jersey 08540

Dear Robert:

I was very moved by your card. It seems to me incredible that a student remembers me after some 25 years and takes the time to tell me that I made a difference in his life. Thanks for taking the time to let me know.

I am very glad to know that you are successful and happy and, most particularly, that you spend your life supporting people with disabilities and have found the way to do that in a large scale. I myself have a niece who has lived since her birth, forty years ago, with a severe case of cerebral palsy. Her mother, who is my sister, started in Mexico City about thirty year ago an organization in favor of people with disabilities, which now has centers all over the country. My sister is still the president of the organization. Since you are in that field, you may have heard of it; its name is APAC.

As for me, I am into my last term of teaching at UD, after 34 years. I’ll be 75 in January, and it is time to make room for younger people, though my course on Existentialism remains as popular as ever.

Well, Robert, thanks again, and may the Lord bless you in your personal and corporate life.

Affectionately yours,

Xavier O. Monasterio
