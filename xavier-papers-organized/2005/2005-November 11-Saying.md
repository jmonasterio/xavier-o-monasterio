2005-November 11-SAYING

SAYING

Searle studies communicative speech, the external speech through which a speaker communicates to a hearer what he means by saying it. Saying is enunciating in words what one wants to commuicate. Language is a system of signs. The uniqueness of language among sings is not that lingjsitic signs mean, for meaning is common to all signs, but that linguistic signs say or enunciate what they mean, and thereby make known what is meant or signified by the speaker.

In order to communicate something, the speaker has to be aware of what he intends to communicate.
