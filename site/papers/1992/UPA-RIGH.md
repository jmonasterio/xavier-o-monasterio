August 26, 1992

Ms. Jennifer Dudley

Editorial Assistant

University Press of America, Inc.

4720 Boston Way

Lanham, MD 20706

Dear Ms Dudley:

I have been referred to you by Ms Julie Burnham, Acquisitions Editor, concerning the following matter.

UPA has agreed to publish a reprint of my book TO BE HUMAN, originally published by Paulist Press. For that purpose I need  permission to quote about 1,200 words from Jean-Paul Sartre's Being and Nothingness , published by Philosophical Library. I had no problem obtaining that permission for the original edition, as verified by the enclosed copy of my contract with Philosophical Library in 1985. My problem now is that, in spite of all my efforts, I have been unable to establish contact with Philosophical Library. I wrote them a letter which was returned to me for lack of a forwarding address, and the telephone call I attempted failed because the number has been disconnected. According to information I obtained from the latest edition of Books in Print , Philosophical Library is out of business.

I think that my good faith in trying to obtain the required permission to quote has been sufficiently proved, and that UPA is entitled to proceed to the reprinting of my book on this basis. In any case and for all useful purposes, I hereby commit myself to pay for the permission to quote if ever a legitimate representative of Philosophical Library appears.

Should you have any questions or further advice to give me concerning this matter, please feel free to contact me.

Sincerely,

Xavier O. Monasterio, Ph. D.
