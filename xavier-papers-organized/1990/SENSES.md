                       Aquinas on Senses of Scripture

1) Literal or  historical:  First  signification,  by  which  words  signify
things.- All the senses are founded on  the  literal.  Because  of  this  no
confusion arises.- Only from the literal sense can an  argument  be  drawn.-
Nothing false can ever underlie the literal sense.
      a) Historical: It is called history when something is simply related.
      b) Etiological:  when  its  cause  (of  what  is  simply  related)  is
assigned. F. e., when it is explained why Moses allowed the putting away  of
wives.
      c) Anagogical: when the truth of one scriptural text is shown  not  to
contradict the truth of another.
      N. B.: The parabolical sense is contained in the  literal,  for  words
can signify things either properly or figuratively.  When  Scripture  speaks
of God's arm, the literal sense is not that God has such a member, but  only
what is signified by this member, namely, operative power.
2) Spiritual or allegorical:  Second  signification,  by  which  the  things
signified by God's words  signify  something  else.-  It  is  based  on  and
presupposes  the  literal  sense.-  Allegory  alone  stands  for  the   tree
spiritual senses:
      a) Allegorical: as far as the old law  signifies  things  of  the  new
law.
      b) Moral: As fars as the things which signify Christ  are  also  signs
of what we ought to do.
      c) Analogical: as far as they signify what relates to eternal glory.
