1) Meursault eats, makes love and sleeps whenever he feels like it, and Camus finds him admirable. Dogs do the same. Would Camus find dogs admirable? Why, or why not?

2) Meursault could have lied to the Magistrate in order to save his life. Admittedly, for Meursault to do that would have been out of character. But would that have contradicted Camus’ philosophy? Explain.

3) Why does Camus call religious faith “philosophical suicide”? And do you agree with him on this point?

4) Would Camus say that anyone who disagrees with him as to the absurdity of life is inauthentic?

Explain why, or why not.

5) The person who realizes the absurdity of life may opt either for quantity or for quality.

What did Meursault opt for? Explain.
