AdSkin-Sa

1) They would not mean the same. Skinner would mean that we change because the environment conditions us to change, whereas Sartre means that we change because we choose to change.

2) No . Only acquired behavior depends on consequences. The basic physiological reflexes are not acquired, they are hereditary . The pupil exposed to light contracts no matter the consequences that may follow the contraction.

3) What the rat says is that it is conditioning Skinner (as much as Skinner is conditioning the rat).

Skinner would have to agree with the rat. He does not see any fundamental difference between animals and people. The fact that the rat walks in circles follows Skinner's behavior of feeding the rat and acts as a positive consequence. So Skinner will tend to continue feeding the rat.

Sartre would disagree . In people existence precedes essence.

Skinner is a person. He can stop feeding the rat any time he chooses to do so, whereas the rat cannot choose to stop walking in circles.

4) Sincerity is a form of bad faith that consists in acknowledging what one is but without assuming responsibility for it, as if what one is were a destiny rather than a role one chooses to play.

The statement is not correct , for it says that "any" student like the one described is sincere. But that is not the case. A student may acknowledge that he/she is lazy because that is what he/she has chosen to be and may choose to continue being lazy. In that case, the student is authentic, not sincere, since he/she assumes responsibility for what he/she is.  

5) Sartre would say that only the Alphas can escape their conditioning, if they start thinking reflectively, as Mustapha Mond did. The Epsilons cannot escape their conditioning, since they were "deprived of human intelligence." Therefore they cannot think in any way, either functionally or reflectively.
