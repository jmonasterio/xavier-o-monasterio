Course Number : PHL 451-01
Title: Seminar in Individual Philosophers: SARTRE

CHARACTERIZATION

      An in-depth  study  of  the  most  important  chapters  of  Being  and
Nothingness, combined with the study of  the  most  representative  literary
works written by Sartre during the same period: Nausea, The Flies, No  Exit,
and Dirty Hands. The course  will  be  completed  with  a  general  idea  of
Sartre's philosophical evolution from Being and Nothingness to the  Critique
of  Dialectical  Reason.  Some  articles  will  be  added  for  purposes  of
discussion of particular aspects of Sartre's philosophy.

CLASSROOM PROCEDURES

       Background  lectures  and  seminar  discussion,  possibly  with  some
presentations by students, depending on number of students.

EVALUATION TELCHNIQUES

      On each of the literary works there will be a short test.
The rest of the grade will be determined on the basis of two exams  and  one
paper.
