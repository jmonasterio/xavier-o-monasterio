Ex-98-02    NAME _______________________

(1) Camus demands authenticity. Nevertheless he praises Galileo.

Can these two positions be reconciled with each other, or does Camus contradict himself? Explain.

(2) How does Caligula interpret Cherea's case? And is Caligula's interpretation correct, given the facts we get in the play? Explain.

(3) Assuming that God doesn't exist and, therefore, that plants are not created by God, could Sartre meaningfully say that in plants existence precedes essence? Explain.

(4) Can the theme of "forlornness" be found in both the atheistic and the Christian existentialists? Explain.

(5) Do you consider yourself an existentialist? Why, or why not?
